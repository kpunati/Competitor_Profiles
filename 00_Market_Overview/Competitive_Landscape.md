# Competitive Landscape

*The 48 firms organized into clusters by structural pattern, with each cluster's strengths, weaknesses, and notable examples called out on their own terms.*

*Synthesized 2026-06-17.*

---

## The map

Three axes describe most of the variance across the catalog:

1. **Pricing model** — AUM-only · subscription/flat-fee · advice-only · revenue-share
2. **Distribution model** — founder-led independent · PE-backed roll-up · content-led platform · B2B network · vendor adjacency
3. **Service surface** — generalist (one-stop financial planning) · niche-specialist (segment, occupation, life-event, or geographic) · platform/aggregator

Most firms fit cleanly into one cluster. The interesting strategic information lives in the **outliers** that don't, and in the **patterns the clusters share or contrast on**.

## The clusters

### Cluster 1: PE-backed national roll-ups

- **Who's in it**: [Mercer Advisors](../Competitors/Mercer_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ($84B), [Carson Group](../Competitors/Carson_Group/1_Summary.md) ($51B), [EP Wealth Advisors](../Competitors/Ep_Wealth_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ($45B), [The Mather Group](../Competitors/The_Mather_Group/1_Summary.md) ($13B), [Focus Partners](../Competitors/Focus_Partners/1_Summary.md)
- **Strengths**: Capital backing for aggressive acquisition pipelines; multi-publication media recognition (Barron's, Forbes, etc.); standardized operations across acquired branches; consolidated tech + compliance stacks
- **Weaknesses**: Brand consolidation friction (acquired firms lose their personality); growth pressure from PE owners requires continued acquisition or upmarket movement; anonymous large-firm feel — most lead with logos rather than people; complex governance limits responsiveness
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: M&A as a stated business line ([Mather Group](../Competitors/The_Mather_Group/1_Summary.md) puts "M&A for RIAs" in main nav). Media-recognition logo walls as a primary credibility signal. Brand-rebrand discipline ([Mather's](../Competitors/The_Mather_Group/1_Summary.md) clean transition from Sebold).

### Cluster 2: Employee-owned + private-partnership independent firms

- **Who's in it**: [Modera Wealth Management](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) ($17.5B, NJ, since 1983, employee-owned), [Pure Financial Advisors](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ($11B, San Diego), [Plancorp](../Competitors/Plancorp/1_Summary.md) ($9.9B, St. Louis), [Truepoint Wealth Counsel](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md) ($6.4B, Cincinnati), [Burney](../Competitors/Burney_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) ($3.6B, Reston VA), [Compass Ion Advisors](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ($1.6B, PA)
- **Strengths**: Refusing to sell *is* a positioning move that the roll-ups cannot match. Long-history brand assets (Modera's 1983 timeline, Truepoint's "since 1990"). Talent retention story (advisors who don't want to be rolled-up). Deeper local/regional relationships.
- **Weaknesses**: Steady-not-fast growth (no acquisition capital, organic-only); brand voice across cluster often lands in cliché territory (Plancorp opens with "holistic and comprehensive"); pricing transparency uneven within cluster.
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md)'s 4-vs-4 anti-wirehouse contrast (the catalog's strongest copywriting). [Modera](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md)'s timeline-as-brand-asset (years of growth listed as marketing material). [Compass Ion](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md)'s "Guide for Good" named content franchise.

### Cluster 3: Niche-specialist mid-tier firms

- **Who's in it**: [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) ($563M, tech + creatives), [Cordant Wealth Partners](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) ($370M, PNW tech employees), [Greenbush Financial Group](../Competitors/Greenbush_Financial_Group/1_Summary.md) ($617M, NY + 401(k)), [Foundry Financial](../Competitors/Foundry_Financial/1_Summary.md) ($95M, LA), [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) ($185M, TX retirement), [Wiser Wealth Management](../Competitors/Wiser_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) ($514M, GA 6-niche), [Walser Wealth](../Competitors/Walser_Wealth/1_Summary.md), [Advize Wealth](../Competitors/Advize_Wealth/1_Summary.md)
- **Strengths**: Segment commitment delivers sharper messaging. Credential-niche pairing ([Brooklyn FI's](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) ECA = Equity Compensation Associate on every planner) signals real niche commitment. Content cadence aligned to niche audience.
- **Weaknesses**: Concentration risk — when the niche cools (tech IPO windows, layoff cycles), pipeline thins. Brand confined to the niche limits cross-segment expansion. Most have not published pricing.
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md)'s three productized tiers (Start Up / Core / Private with AUM thresholds). [Cordant](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md)'s explicit naming of employer clients (Intel / Microsoft / Nike / Amazon / Apple). [Greenbush](../Competitors/Greenbush_Financial_Group/1_Summary.md)'s 401(k) consulting as a homepage pillar. [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md)'s three named ICP personas on the homepage.

### Cluster 4: Subscription / advice-only challengers

- **Who's in it**: [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) ($43M AUM, VC-funded — Bessemer/SV Angel/Maveron, LPs include Ashton Kutcher + Marc Benioff, advisor board chaired by Joe Duran of United Capital), [Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($51M AUM, HENRY niche), [Abundo Wealth](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($0 AUM by design, 12 advisors, advice-only)
- **Strengths**: Published pricing as the lead (transparency is their wedge). Lower CAC for younger prospects. Subscription unit economics work without AUM scale. Mission framing ([Abundo](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md)'s "change the financial planning industry") gives recruiting + brand advantages.
- **Weaknesses**: No compounding revenue per client (AUM-based firms capture upside as clients' wealth grows; subscription firms don't). Subscription churn is the existential risk — Year-1 to renewal transition determines LTV. Customer acquisition cost is high without VC backing (Domain Money's economics are uncertain at exit-stage).
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md)'s Year-1 vs. renewal pricing structure. [Stash](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md)'s "HENRY" generational niche label. [Abundo](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md)'s advice-only-as-the-product positioning.

### Cluster 5: Content-led personality firms

- **Who's in it**: [Money Guy](../Competitors/Money_Guy/1_Summary.md), [Afford Anything](../Competitors/Afford_Anything/1_Summary.md), [Ritholtz Wealth](../Competitors/Ritholtz_Wealth/1_Summary.md), [Karlton Dennis](../Competitors/Karlton_Dennis/1_Summary.md), [ClearValue Investing](../Competitors/Clearvalue_Investing/1_Summary.md), [The Peak FP](../Competitors/The_Peak_Fp/1_Summary.md), [Sven Carlin](../Competitors/Sven_Carlin/1_Summary.md), [PWL Capital](../Competitors/Pwl_Capital/1_Summary.md)
- **Strengths**: Content surface (podcast / YouTube / blog) is the brand and the funnel simultaneously. Audience size compounds. Brand reach exceeds AUM by 10x or more in most cases.
- **Weaknesses**: Single-personality key-person risk (the brand collapses if the founder steps back). Content cadence discipline required indefinitely. Advisory revenue is downstream of audience — economic dependency on platforms (YouTube, podcast distribution) the firm doesn't control.
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: Personality-driven origin stories as positioning anchors. Long-form content as the primary trust-builder. Named franchise discipline ([Money Guy](../Competitors/Money_Guy/1_Summary.md)'s show, [PWL Capital](../Competitors/Pwl_Capital/1_Summary.md)'s Rational Reminder podcast).

### Cluster 6: B2B platforms / advisor networks / co-ops

- **Who's in it**: [XY Planning Network](../Competitors/Xy_Planning_Network/1_Summary.md), [Avise Financial](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md) (RIA co-op), [Focus Partners](../Competitors/Focus_Partners/1_Summary.md) (also Cluster 1)
- **Strengths**: Scale economics on ops + compliance + tech for member advisors. [Avise](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md)'s tiered revenue-share (15% → 10% → 5%) genuinely rewards advisor growth. Cooperative governance ([Avise](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md)'s patronage dividends) is structurally aligned in a way corporate platforms can't replicate.
- **Weaknesses**: Consumer-facing funnel competes with member firms; the platform front captures prospects but member firms get the relationship. Hard to brand at the platform level when individual member advisors have their own brands.
- **What they do that's worth understanding**: Member-firm pricing transparency (e.g., [Avise](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md) publishes outsourced ops at $45-$70/hr). Cooperative governance as a structural alternative to PE-backed roll-up exits.

### Cluster 7: Vendor adjacencies + edge cases

- **Who's in it**: [Asset Map](../Competitors/Asset_Map/1_Summary.md) (advisor software), [Couplr AI](../Competitors/Couplr_AI/1_Summary.md) (matching tech), [Kitces](../Competitors/Kitces/1_Summary.md) (advisor education), [Tax Alchemy](../Competitors/Tax_Alchemy/1_Summary.md), [KDA Inc](../Competitors/Kda_Inc/1_Summary.md), [My CPA Coach](../Competitors/My_Cpa_Coach/1_Summary.md), [Heritage Wealth Planning](../Competitors/Heritage_Wealth_Planning/1_Summary.md), [Roadmap Money](../Competitors/Roadmap_Money/1_Summary.md), [Synergy Private](../Competitors/Synergy_Private/1_Summary.md), [Peoples Capital Group](../Competitors/Peoples_Capital_Group/1_Summary.md), [Starke](../Competitors/Starke/1_Summary.md), [Samo Financial](../Competitors/Samo_Financial/1_Summary.md), [Merit Financial Advisors](../Competitors/Merit_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md), [Dealmakers](../Competitors/Dealmakers/1_Summary.md), [Intrepid Wealth Partners](../Competitors/Intrepid_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md), [Streamline Planning](../Competitors/Streamline_Planning/1_Summary.md)
- **Strengths**: Lower direct-competition pressure (different end-buyer or product); often more specialized expertise in a single domain (tax-only, education, vendor tooling).
- **Weaknesses**: Variable quality across the cluster; some stale/dormant; some are vendor-positioning that's unclear or evolving.
- **Useful for**: pattern-matching specific functions (e.g., [Streamline Planning](../Competitors/Streamline_Planning/1_Summary.md)'s pricing transparency, [Intrepid Wealth Partners](../Competitors/Intrepid_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md)'s tier-based AUM table).

## The outliers

Firms whose strategy doesn't fit cleanly in their cluster — usually the most interesting strategic information lives here.

- **[Burney](../Competitors/Burney_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md)** — active-management at $3.59B in a cluster dominated by passive/indexed firms. Proves the active methodology scales past $1B without sacrificing the message.
- **[Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md)** — single-founder personality firm that owns a generational niche label. Brand reach far outweighs AUM, suggesting the niche-label move has economic value independent of book scale.
- **[Abundo Wealth](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md)** — 12 advisors operating at $0 regulatory AUM. Activist mission framing. Proves subscription-only economics work past solo-founder scale.
- **[Avise Financial](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md)** — RIA co-op. Democratic governance + patronage dividends. Genuinely novel structural alternative to the corporate-platform model XYPN/Focus run.
- **[Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md)** — the catalog's strongest copywriting (4-vs-4 anti-wirehouse contrast) executed at $6.4B AUM. Proves bold brand voice and large firm-scale are not in tension.
- **[Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md)** — VC-funded + repeat-founder pedigree + Goldman-adjacent advisor board. The most credible challenger to traditional RIAs the catalog contains.

## Momentum

- **Accelerating**: [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) (VC capital, 39 employees, recent product expansion), [Carson Group](../Competitors/Carson_Group/1_Summary.md) (continued PE-led acquisitions), [Mercer](../Competitors/Mercer_Advisors/1_Summary.md) (largest acquisition warchest in the catalog), [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) (registered ~2018 → ~21-person team + $563M AUM in ~7 years)
- **Steady scale-up**: [Modera](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) (10+ acquisitions 1995-2024 per their /our-story timeline), [Plancorp](../Competitors/Plancorp/1_Summary.md), [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md), [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md)
- **Repositioning**: [The Mather Group](../Competitors/The_Mather_Group/1_Summary.md) (rebranded from Sebold — deliberate roll-up brand consolidation)
- **Stuck or stale**: [Roadmap Money](../Competitors/Roadmap_Money/1_Summary.md) (content cadence dropped), [Heritage Wealth Planning](../Competitors/Heritage_Wealth_Planning/1_Summary.md) (3 posts visible), [Samo Financial](../Competitors/Samo_Financial/1_Summary.md) (100% Cloudflare-blocked capture)
