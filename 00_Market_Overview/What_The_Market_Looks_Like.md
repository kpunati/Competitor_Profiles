# What The Market Looks Like

*Patterns across the 48-firm catalog — what every firm does, what only some do, what the entire market is missing, and where each pattern is strong or weak.*

*Synthesized 2026-06-17.*

---

## Table stakes — patterns so common they're expected

Doing these doesn't differentiate. Skipping them stands out for the wrong reasons.

### Visual + structural

- Clean modern design — gold/navy or earth-tone palette, professional advisor photography, single-column hero with center-aligned tagline
- Five-section sitemap: Home / About / Services / Insights or Blog / Contact (some add Our Process / FAQ / Resources)
- "Schedule a Consultation" or "Book a Call" CTA in the upper-right of the nav
- Form CRS link in the footer
- LinkedIn icon in nav (YouTube/Instagram added by content-led firms)

### Verbal

- "Fee-only fiduciary" — claimed by every fee-only firm. Legally meaningful but not by itself a positioning move.
- "Comprehensive wealth management" — used by [Plancorp](../Competitors/Plancorp/1_Summary.md), [Modera](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md), and most large peers. Generic. Strong only because peers are using it.
- "Personalized" or "tailored to your needs" — filler.
- "Helping you achieve your financial goals" — generic outcome promise; doesn't say what the firm does that anyone else doesn't.
- "Your trusted partner" — used by ~15+ firms in the catalog.

### Pitch architecture

- 4-step process diagram: Discovery → Plan → Implement → Review
- Testimonial carousel mid-homepage (text + headshot; some include video)
- Logo wall: "As seen in" Barron's / Forbes / WSJ / CNBC — heaviest on the largest firms ([The Mather Group](../Competitors/The_Mather_Group/1_Summary.md), [Mercer](../Competitors/Mercer_Advisors/1_Summary.md), [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md))
- About / Team page with cropped headshots + credential strings + 1-2 paragraph bios beginning *"[Name] brings 20+ years of experience..."*
- FAQ section that answers easy questions (how do you charge, what's your minimum) but not hard ones (objections, comparison points, "why not robo")

## Differentiators — patterns that set certain firms apart

Each differentiator is paired with **why it works** and **where the pattern is brittle**.

| Pattern | Examples | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Published tier pricing** | [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) (Start Up / Core / Private with AUM thresholds), [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) ($3.9K-$9K Yr-1 vs. $2.9K-$8K renewal), [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md), [Streamline Planning](../Competitors/Streamline_Planning/1_Summary.md) | Eliminates the "schedule a call to learn pricing" friction that loses comparison-shopping prospects. Concrete numbers win head-to-head when peer firms gate them. | Locks the firm to a price point publicly. Re-pricing requires deliberate brand work. Discounting becomes harder. |
| **In-house tax bench** | [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) (5 CPAs, CPA Director), [EP Wealth](../Competitors/Ep_Wealth_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("integrate your taxes and estate" tagline) | Bundles a service most peers refer out. Captures revenue + relationship depth the referral model loses. | Operationally heavier — actual tax-prep season has compliance + capacity constraints. Most firms outsource for good reason. |
| **Named content franchise** *(vs. generic "Blog")* | [Compass Ion](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("Guide for Good"), [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("Your Money Your Wealth" podcast), [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) ("Nick's Weekly Memo") | Names the content surface as a positioning asset. Compounds brand equity over time. | Requires cadence discipline. A franchise that goes 3 months silent feels worse than no franchise. |
| **Founder origin story as anchor** | [Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) (ballerina → Merrill → founder), [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) (10-year pastor → CFP), [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) (Goldman/Dell/Autodesk exits) | An irreducible credibility story competitors can't copy. Emotional anchor for prospect trust. | Single-key-person risk if founder steps back. Story has to be genuinely distinctive — most founder bios aren't. |
| **Explicit anti-wirehouse contrast** | [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md) (4-vs-4 hero table — *"People · Compassion · Your Trust · Return on Life"* vs. *"Products · Commissions · Our Bottom Line · Return on Investment"*), [Cordant](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) ("not constrained by the whims of a Wall Street bank"), [Avise](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md) ("financial corporations spend hundreds of millions on advertising") | Direct rhetoric earns trust faster than polite differentiation. Works across firm size and age. | Requires conviction at the brand level — half-hearted versions read defensive. |
| **Generational niche label** | [Stash](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) (HENRY = High Earner, Not Rich Yet) | Audience self-identifies. Naming the cohort is itself a positioning move. | Lifecycle risk — a label that fits 30-somethings now ages out in 10 years. |
| **Geographic specificity by employer** | [Cordant](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) names Intel / Microsoft / Nike / Amazon / Apple as target employer base | Prospect self-identifies immediately. Beats generic "tech professionals." | Concentration risk — when a named employer cools (layoffs, M&A), the segment narrows fast. |
| **Free education event as top-of-funnel** | [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("Attend a Retirement Class" alongside "Talk to an Advisor") | Lower-stakes entry that captures email + qualified-lead intent before the consultation conversation. | Class-format requires recurring schedule + content production overhead. |
| **Long-history brand asset** | [Modera](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) (1983 timeline with year-by-year milestones), [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md) ("since 1990"), [Burney](../Competitors/Burney_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) | Credibility shortcut. *"Since 1990"* does positioning work no younger firm can match. | Only available to firms with actual history. Underused by firms that *do* have it. |
| **Multi-credential founder pairing** | [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) (AJ Ayers CFP/EA/CEP + Shane Mason CPA/PFS/CFP/CPWA) | Complementary credentials cover blind spots a single-credential founder can't. Doubles as a recruiting signal. | Two founders = two compensation expectations. Cap-table complexity vs. solo founders. |
| **Year-1 vs. renewal pricing** | [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) ($3.9K Yr-1 / $2.9K renewal) | Reflects that initial plan-build is heavy work; renewals are maintenance. Psychologically softens the Year-1 sticker shock. | Renewal-vs.-Year-1 framing only works if delivery actually differs across years. |
| **Subscription as the product** *(not a footnote)* | [Abundo Wealth](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($0 AUM by design, 12 advisors), [Stash](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($5K flat-fee Plan), [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) | Captures prospects with portfolios too small to justify AUM. Cleaner unit economics at the entry tier. | No compounding revenue from successful clients (AUM doubles → fee doesn't). Churn is the structural risk. |
| **Mission framing** | [Abundo](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ("we're on a mission to change the financial planning industry") | Activist language unusual for advisory firms. Recruiting advantage for advisors leaving AUM-only firms. | Hard to back up. A mission stated and not lived reads as marketing. |
| **401(k) consulting as a named pillar** | [Greenbush](../Competitors/Greenbush_Financial_Group/1_Summary.md) (Company Retirement Plans as homepage pillar #2) | Captures a cohort competitors treat as a side-service. Concrete answer to a real anxiety the 40-50 cohort has. | Plan-sponsor work is operationally different from retail. The pillar only works if the firm actually does the work. |

## Anti-patterns — common moves that hurt the firm doing them

Patterns the catalog reveals as **executed badly more often than not**, even when each individual instance was a reasonable choice on paper.

- **"Our Values" with three generic icons** (Integrity / Excellence / Client-first). Used by many firms. Differentiates none. The catalog's strongest brands attach **specific behaviors** to convictions instead — e.g. [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md)'s 4-vs-4 contrast names what they reject, not what they aspire to.
- **WordPress default meta description in production.** [Compass Ion Advisors](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ($1.59B AUM) ships their homepage with the WordPress placeholder ("This is an example page. It's different from a blog post... I'm a bike messenger by day..."). Signals that marketing ops is undermanaged.
- **"Comprehensive" + "holistic" as the lead.** [Plancorp](../Competitors/Plancorp/1_Summary.md) ($9.94B) opens with both — neither word differentiates and both are on most fee-only RIAs' opt-in vocabulary. Strong only because peers also use it, which is the definition of an anti-pattern.
- **No published pricing.** ~80% of the catalog. Reasonable defensively (sales conversations close better when prospects ask price); structurally weak when prospects are comparison-shopping against the 20% that *do* publish.
- **Multi-niche menu strategy.** [Wiser Wealth Management](../Competitors/Wiser_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) lists six niches on the homepage (Retirement / Business Owners / Single Again / Airline Pilots / Legacy / Inheritance). Each one has its own funnel, but the brand-level positioning gets diluted.
- **Branded but absent content surface.** A blog link with nothing posted in 6+ months. Catalog has several. Signals lack of cadence discipline — worse than no blog at all.
- **Anonymous credibility claims.** "Decades of combined experience" / "our team of experts" — the catalog has dozens of instances. Specific named credentials (CFP®, CFA, EA, CPWA®) attached to specific people convert. Anonymized aggregate claims don't.

## Whitespace — what the entire market is missing

These are gaps across the catalog regardless of who tries to fill them.

- **Active management + transparent published pricing.** [Burney](../Competitors/Burney_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) ($3.59B) is the catalog's largest active-management firm and does not publish fees. The combination is empty.
- **Truly entry-level first-investor product.** [Stash](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) targets HENRYs (six-figure 30-somethings); [Abundo](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) says "For Everyone" but is broad; [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md)'s Essential at $2,900/yr renewal is still too high for a $5K starter portfolio. No catalog firm publishes a $500-$2,500 flat-fee onboarding product for the just-starting-out cohort.
- **Named 401(k) optimization for the mid-career individual** (not plan-sponsor). [Greenbush](../Competitors/Greenbush_Financial_Group/1_Summary.md) is the closest, but addresses plan sponsors more than individual mid-career participants with corporate 401(k)s + held-away IRAs.
- **Wirehouse-defection origin story** that pairs with active-management methodology. The catalog has founder origin stories ([Stash](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md)'s ballerina arc, [Brindle](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md)'s pastor arc) and active-management firms ([Burney](../Competitors/Burney_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md), [Modera](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md)), but no firm leans on a *wirehouse-to-independent founder arc* as the active-management credibility anchor.
- **Three-conviction operating philosophy.** Most firms have generic value triplets. Convictions with **specific behaviors attached** (the format USWM uses per `_context/brand_voice.md` — *"the trend is your friend / risk first, returns second / we'll tell you if not a fit"*) are essentially absent.
- **Honest hybrid pricing.** Most firms are AUM-only or pure subscription/flat. The transparent middle — flat-fee for planning + AUM for managed assets, clearly explained — is rare.
- **Anti-wirehouse rhetoric with actual wirehouse departure as the source.** [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md), [Cordant](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md), [Avise](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md) all use the rhetoric but anchor it on institutional opinion. A firm with a founder who actually left Merrill / JPMorgan / Citi / UBS / AIG and uses that history publicly would carry the framing more credibly than any of them.
