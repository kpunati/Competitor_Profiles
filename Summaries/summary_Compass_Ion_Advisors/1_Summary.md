# Compass Ion Advisors — Summary

> PA-based fee-only fiduciary RIA — **$1.59B AUM, 24 employees, 15 advisors, 0 DRPs.** Tagline: *"Live freely."* Six-section site IA (Mission · People · Process · Services · Guide for Good · Insights). Solid mid-large regional firm targeting HNW/UHNW. **Notable quality marker**: their homepage `<meta description>` is the **WordPress default placeholder** text ("This is an example page... I'm a bike messenger by day…") — they shipped the site without setting it. For a $1.59B AUM firm, that's a real signal about marketing-ops attention.

## The headline

Compass Ion is **3x larger by AUM than the next-largest fee-only firm in this batch (Brooklyn FI at $563M)**, and they target the HNW/UHNW tier rather than first-time investors or HENRYs. They're not a direct USWM competitor — their wealth tier is one level above ours, and their geography (PA) doesn't overlap. Strategic value is mostly as a benchmark of what a fully-built mid-large RIA looks like at $1B+: 24 employees, six-section nav, named "Guide for Good" content franchise, "Live freely" tagline that frames the entire firm around a single 2-word promise.

The WordPress meta default left in production is a small but meaningful tell about how the firm runs day-to-day marketing.

## What they sell, to whom

Six-section IA: **Mission · People · Process · Services · Guide for Good · Insights.** The "Guide for Good" is the differentiator — a named content franchise that frames their financial advice through a values lens. "Live freely" is the operating promise. Pricing not disclosed on the captured pages.

ICP appears to be **HNW/UHNW families** (the $106M-per-advisor book size implies $5-15M average accounts).

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 2 ($1.59B AUM solid, content franchise + insights surface), Segment overlap 1 (HNW/UHNW PA-based — doesn't compete for USWM's early-adult or 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segments), Momentum 2 (multi-section IA + content + 15 advisors), Capital backing 1 (private partnership) → **6 → low**.

## What they're doing well

- **"Live freely" as a 2-word promise.** Tagline doubles as the site headline and the closing CTA frame. Consistent through 5 pages.
- **"Guide for Good" content franchise.** Named, branded content series — a step beyond generic "Blog" or "Insights." Makes the content surface a positioning asset, not just a SEO asset.
- **Six-section IA.** Mission · People · Process · Services · Guide for Good · Insights. Each section is a real page, not a marketing dropdown. Suggests committed information architecture.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **WordPress default meta in production.** Small thing, real signal. Site search results show placeholder text.
- **No published pricing.** Same gap as Cordant and Greenbush.
- **Generic positioning at the top.** "Live freely" is evocative but mass-market vague. No segment-specific language captured.

## What this means for our entry

1. **A named content franchise > a generic blog.** "Guide for Good" beats "Insights" beats "Blog." For USWM, a named content series ("Real Conviction: Notes from the Other Side of Wall Street" or similar) gives the content a positioning function.
2. **Audit your own site meta before launch.** Even $1.59B AUM firms ship WordPress default placeholders. USWM's pre-launch checklist should include a deliberate meta + OG image pass.

The narrowest single takeaway: **brand the content surface.** A named franchise out-performs a generic blog header on day one.
