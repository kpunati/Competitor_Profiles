# Greenbush Financial Group — Summary

> Capital District NY fee-only fiduciary RIA — **$616.5M AUM concentrated in 3 advisors / 6 employees** (high per-advisor book ~$205M each). Three service pillars on the homepage: **Investment Management · Company Retirement Plans · Financial Planning.** Strong tax-expertise emphasis. 0 DRPs. Of the 18-firm batch, has the most relevant **401(k) angle** for USWM's 40-50 mid-career segment. Content-led with active blog. Pricing not disclosed on homepage.

## The headline

Greenbush is a **3-advisor firm that runs $616M of AUM** — that's roughly the same per-advisor book as a typical wirehouse senior at a Goldman or JPMorgan. It's small in headcount but large in book size, which means each client is a substantial relationship (likely $1-10M+ accounts). The differentiator on the homepage is **Company Retirement Plans** as a co-equal service to Investment Management — most fee-only RIAs treat 401(k) as a side service; Greenbush makes it pillar #2.

For USWM's 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment, Greenbush is the closest thing in this 18-firm batch to a structural peer in *how they think about 401(k) work*. They're not a direct competitor (different region, different wealth tier — Greenbush is mostly HNW), but the messaging architecture is borrowable.

## Who they are

- **Founded**: Pre-2003 (CRD 125500 vintage)
- **Size**: 6 employees, 3 advisors per Form ADV
- **HQ**: New York Capital District (Albany/Saratoga area)
- **Funding**: Founder-led
- **Regulatory**: SEC-registered (CRD 125500), 0 DRPs
- **AUM**: $616,539,856 as of 2026-06-17

## What they sell, to whom

Three service pillars, each on the homepage:
1. **Investment Management** — primary
2. **Company Retirement Plans** — employer-sponsored 401(k) consulting (unusually prominent)
3. **Financial Planning**

Plus: Tax Expertise, Virtual Financial Planning & Investment Management. Site emphasizes "Our Client Relationships Start With a Financial Plan" — planning-first conversion logic. Pricing not on homepage.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 2 ($616M AUM is solid, blog + tax + 401(k) surfaces visible), Segment overlap 2 (Company Retirement Plans is a real angle into our 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment, but their actual delivery is mostly to plan sponsors not individual participants), Momentum 2 (active content + virtual delivery), Capital backing 1 (founder-led) → **7 → low**, with the caveat that the 401(k) angle is the most segment-relevant of any firm in this batch.

## What they're doing well

- **Company Retirement Plans as a co-equal pillar.** Pillar #2 on the homepage, equal weight to investment management. For USWM's 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment, this positioning logic is directly borrowable: don't treat 401(k) as a side service; make it equal billing with investment management.
- **"Virtual Financial Planning & Investment Management"** explicit as a service line. Most regional firms hedge on virtual; Greenbush makes it a named service.
- **Tax Expertise as a stated specialty.** USWM has Beata — Greenbush's framing of tax as a stated capability (not just a referral) is the pattern.
- **Subscribe to Blog visible.** Content engine in operation.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **No published pricing.** Behind 80% of the catalog on transparency.
- **3-advisor team running $616M is brittle.** Key-person risk per advisor is enormous; one departure = significant book at risk.
- **No segment-specific landing pages visible.** Three pillars in one homepage row; no dedicated landing pages for the 40-50 401(k) cohort that's their best segment fit.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Treat Company Retirement Plans as a USWM pillar, not a footnote.** Greenbush proves the positioning works for a $616M firm. USWM's 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment deserves a named, prominent service line on the homepage — not buried under "Financial Planning."
2. **Tax Expertise as a stated capability.** Make Beata's EA + NATP the public-facing answer to "do you do tax?" Greenbush leans on this framing without a credentialed tax person on-staff (likely outsourced or referred); USWM has the real credential.

The narrowest single takeaway: **make 401(k) consulting a named pillar in USWM's site IA, equal-weight to Investment Management.**
