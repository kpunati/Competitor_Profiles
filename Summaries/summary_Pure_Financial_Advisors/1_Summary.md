# Pure Financial Advisors — Summary

> San Diego fee-only fiduciary RIA — **$11.05B AUM, 204 employees, 76 advisors, 0 DRPs.** Retirement-focused with major content brand ("Your Money Your Wealth" podcast). Strong site IA (sitemap=300, nav=94). Two homepage CTAs: **"Attend a Retirement Class"** (free education funnel) + **"Talk to an Advisor."** Pricing published at /why-pure/fees/. Tagline: "Tax-optimized investment management."

## The headline

Pure Financial is **the catalog's clearest example of a content-led funnel feeding a $10B+ RIA.** "Attend a Retirement Class" is the top-of-funnel — free education event, no hard sell, gathers email + qualified leads. "Your Money Your Wealth" podcast + show extends the content surface beyond the website. Six tiles on the homepage cover Retirement, Tax, Investing, Education — each a real landing page, not a marketing dropdown.

For a 76-advisor / $11B firm to publish a fee schedule at /why-pure/fees/ puts them in the top 20% of catalog peers on transparency. They've decided that fee clarity wins more prospects than it loses.

Threat to USWM: **low** — different region, retirement-skewed cohort doesn't directly compete for early-adult first-investors or our 40-50 mid-career cohort (their 40-50s would be classed as "pre-retirement" and pulled into the retirement funnel).

## What they sell, to whom

Six homepage tiles: **Prepare for Retirement · Reduce Your Tax Liability · Find Your Advisor · Invest for Your Future · Never Stop Learning · Free Resources.** Two primary CTAs: free retirement class + advisor consultation. Pricing published at /why-pure/fees/.

Major content brand: **"Your Money Your Wealth"** podcast/show — known nationally in retirement planning content.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 3 ($11B AUM + major podcast presence), Segment overlap 1 (retirement-focused; 40-50 cohort served as pre-retirement, not as separate segment), Momentum 3 (content engine + free-class funnel + national brand), Capital backing 1 (founder-led) → **8 → low** for USWM's segments specifically.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **"Attend a Retirement Class" as top-of-funnel.** Free education event, no hard sell, captures email + qualified-lead intent. Borrowable structure for USWM at the 40-50 mid-career 401(k) cohort: "Attend our 401(k) Rollover Class."
- **Published fees at /why-pure/fees/.** $11B firm publishing rates. Strong transparency signal.
- **"Your Money Your Wealth" branded content.** Named franchise that scales beyond the website.
- **"Tax-optimized investment management"** as a stated specialty (not just "we do tax").

## Where they're vulnerable

- **204-employee complexity.** Coordination overhead becomes real at this scale.
- **No segment-specific surface for early career or pre-retirement cohorts.** "Prepare for Retirement" is broad — doesn't differentiate the 40-50 vs. 60+ cohorts.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Free education event as funnel.** "Attend our 401(k) Rollover Class" or "First Investment Account Walkthrough" — borrowable structure for USWM's segments. Lower-stakes than booking a consult; captures email; trains the lead before the conversation.
2. **Publish fees.** Pure does it at $11B; USWM should at launch.
3. **Branded podcast/show as long-run content investment.** Not immediate for USWM, but the model is: pick a named franchise ("The USWM Memo" or "Trend & Risk") and run it consistently rather than blog-and-hope.

The narrowest single takeaway: **free-class-as-funnel beats "schedule a consultation" at the top.** Pure proves it works at scale.
