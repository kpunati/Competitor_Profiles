# Domain Money — Summary

> **VC-backed subscription advisory** founded by **Adam Dell** — repeat founder (Clarity Money → Goldman Sachs · MessageOne → Dell · Buzzsaw → Autodesk). **30+ CFP®s on staff, 39 employees, 14 advisors, $43.3M AUM (intentionally low — subscription-first), 0 DRPs.** Three published subscription tiers: **Essential $3,900 yr1/$2,900 renewal · Strategic $5,200/$4,200 ("Most Popular") · Comprehensive $9,000/$8,000.** *"No hidden or AUM fees—ever."* Cancel anytime, 100% satisfaction refund. Advisory board chair: **Joe Duran** (founded United Capital, sold to Goldman). VC: Bessemer · SV Angel · Maveron · Torch · RRE. LPs include **Ashton Kutcher and Marc Benioff.** The most well-capitalized direct competitor to USWM's segments in this batch.

## The headline

Domain Money is **the most threatening firm in this batch for USWM.** They're targeting "the next generation" with a published, transparent, flat-fee subscription model — that *is* USWM's early-adult segment and the entry tier of the 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment. They have **VC capital behind them** (runway to absorb CAC for years), a **repeat founder** who's sold three companies to Goldman / Dell / Autodesk, and an **advisory board chair (Joe Duran) who built United Capital and sold it to Goldman.** This isn't a launch-stage RIA — it's a venture-funded play to disrupt traditional advice.

The Strategic tier at $5,200 (year 1) / $4,200 (renewal) is priced exactly where USWM's 40-50 mid-career 401(k) prospect lives. Comprehensive at $9,000 (renewal $8,000) is competitive with what USWM might charge a $1-3M household on AUM — except Domain Money's price is fixed, regardless of portfolio size.

**Threat: medium.** Not "high" because their AUM is small and they're still proving they can convert subscriptions profitably at scale. Not "low" because they have capital, distribution, founder pedigree, and a directly competing offer.

## How big a threat

**Medium.** **Reach 2** ($43M AUM is low but employee count of 39 + 30+ CFP®s + VC-backed marketing engine = real distribution capacity), **Segment overlap 3** (directly competes for USWM's early adults at Essential tier and 40-50 mid-career at Strategic tier — three tiers cover most of USWM's addressable market), **Momentum 3** (VC capital + Joe Duran advisor board + Marc Benioff and Ashton Kutcher LPs + 30+ CFP®s + product-led growth), **Capital backing 3** (VC-funded with high-tier brand investors) → sum **11 → medium.**

Worth a 90-day review cycle (vs. 180 default) given the trajectory. Could re-rate upward to medium-high if their subscription unit economics work; downward to low-medium if VC pressure forces them upmarket out of USWM's territory.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Published pricing as the lead.** Three tiers, three prices, three "best for" descriptions. Year 1 vs. renewal disclosed. No "schedule a call to learn pricing" — the prices are on the page.
- **"Membership that pays for itself" framing.** Explicit ROI promise on the home page.
- **Year 1 vs. renewal pricing structure.** Higher Year 1 (presumably because the first plan-build is the heavy work), lower renewals. Smart: makes the year-1 onboarding feel justified and the renewal feel like a deal.
- **Joe Duran on the advisory board.** Imports United Capital-grade credibility into a venture-funded play.
- **"No AUM fees—ever" as the differentiator vs. traditional RIAs.** Makes the answer to "why am I paying 1% of my assets when the work is the same regardless of my balance?" concrete.
- **Concierge framing at Comprehensive tier.** Private-wealth-manager language applied at $8,000/yr instead of $50K+/yr.
- **Multi-exit founder.** Adam Dell's pedigree is a credibility shortcut most RIA founders don't have.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **VC pressure on growth.** Bessemer + SV Angel + Maveron need exits. That likely means aggressive customer acquisition + eventual upmarket expansion or aggressive price compression.
- **Subscription churn risk.** Year 1 to renewal transition is the make-or-break moment. A 50% renewal rate at $4,200 vs. $5,200 means LTV is half what naive ARPU math suggests.
- **AUM/employee ratio is broken.** $43M AUM with 39 employees = $1.1M AUM per employee. Traditional RIAs target $50-100M per employee.
- **Key-person risk at founder.** Adam Dell is the brand. If he steps back, the next-generation positioning loses its primary public face.
- **"For the next generation" is generic.** Stash Wealth owns "HENRYs"; Cordant owns "Intel/Microsoft/Nike/Amazon/Apple employees." Domain Money's "next generation" is broader and less self-identifying.

## What this means for our entry

1. **USWM needs to publish at least 2 tiers, ideally 3.** Domain Money's published pricing is the new bar for transparency.
2. **The Year-1 / Renewal pricing structure is a borrowable tactic.** USWM could legitimately charge more in year 1 and less on renewal.
3. **Lean into the AUM-fee + flat-fee hybrid.** Domain Money is purely flat-fee; USWM is purely AUM. The smarter middle: AUM-based for active investment management (USWM's actual differentiator), flat-fee for planning + tax + business advisory engagements. That's a *both/and* positioning neither Domain Money nor traditional AUM RIAs can credibly claim.
4. **Track Domain Money's funding rounds + ADV updates over 12-24 months.** Re-rate threat upward if they start meaningfully scaling AUM.

The narrowest single takeaway: **publish a tier table with Year-1 vs. Renewal prices.** Domain Money's transparency is the new minimum bar; opaque-pricing RIAs will lose head-to-head comparisons even when their service is superior.
