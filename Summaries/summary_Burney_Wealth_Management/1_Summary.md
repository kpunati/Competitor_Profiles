# Burney Company — Summary

> Reston VA fee-only fiduciary RIA serving **both retail (individuals/families) and institutional clients** — **$3.59B AUM, 46 employees, 42 advisors, 0 DRPs.** Legal name **THE BURNEY COMPANY** (originally on the candidate list as "Burney Wealth Management" — names diverge). Active-management firm — **closer to USWM's investment philosophy than most large RIAs**, which lean passive/indexed. Dual-brand: burney.com (family/wealth side) + burneyinvest.com (institutional asset management).

## The headline

Burney is the catalog's clearest **active-management RIA at meaningful scale** — they explicitly do not lean on indexed / passive portfolios the way Modera and Plancorp implicitly do. The dual structure (family/wealth on burney.com, institutional asset management on burneyinvest.com) means the firm's investment process is rigorous enough to serve institutional mandates *and* retail families. For USWM's trend-following + active investment management positioning, Burney is the credibility benchmark — they prove an active-management RIA can scale past $3.5B.

42 advisors at $3.59B AUM = ~$85M per advisor. Mid-large book size. Threat to USWM's segments: low (different region, HNW/institutional tier).

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 3 ($3.59B AUM), Segment overlap 1 (HNW/institutional, no USWM-segment overlap), Momentum 2 (national reach, dual-market), Capital backing 1 (private partnership) → **7 → low**.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Burney proves active-management RIA scales.** Most large fee-only RIAs in the catalog lean passive/indexed (Modera, Plancorp, Mercer's typical book). Burney's active-investment approach + $3.59B AUM = USWM's strategic-credibility benchmark. When USWM defends "active investment management" against an indexer's "you can't beat the market" objection, Burney is one of the names to cite.
2. **Dual-brand structure is sophisticated but cautious.** burney.com vs. burneyinvest.com keeps the consumer audience from confusion about the institutional offering. USWM might eventually want the same separation if we go institutional, but for launch stage, one brand is the right answer.

The narrowest single takeaway: **active-management at scale is real** — Burney's existence is evidence USWM's positioning isn't a niche-only stance.
