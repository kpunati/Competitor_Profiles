# The Mather Group — Summary

> Chicago-based **national RIA roll-up** (formerly **Sebold Capital Management** before rebrand) — **$13.4B AUM, 186 employees, 0 DRPs.** Tagline: *"Why-powered wealth management."* Active M&A acquirer — nav includes **"M&A for RIAs"**. Recognized by Barron's, Financial Advisor, and Top 100 RIA lists. Several orders of magnitude larger than USWM; useful primarily as a **"what a roll-up at scale looks like"** benchmark rather than direct competitor.

## The headline

The Mather Group is **the largest firm in the 18-firm batch by AUM** ($13.4B), 186 employees deep, and the most aggressive M&A acquirer in the catalog. The rebrand from "Sebold Capital Management" to "The Mather Group" suggests deliberate roll-up brand consolidation. The "M&A for RIAs" nav item makes their acquirer-of-choice positioning explicit — they're recruiting smaller RIAs to join them. This is the structural option USWM faces if our growth path leads us to a sale: Mather is on the buyer list, alongside Mercer, Focus, EP, and Carson.

Threat to USWM as a competitor: **low** — they target HNW/UHNW families, not first-time investors or mid-career 401(k) holders. They don't appear on the consideration set for USWM's two target segments.

## Who they are

- **Founded**: Pre-2010 (CRD 156005 vintage)
- **Size**: 186 employees per Form ADV; advisor count not parsed
- **HQ**: Chicago, IL
- **Funding**: PE-backed (typical structure for an RIA roll-up at this scale)
- **Regulatory**: SEC-registered (CRD 156005), 0 DRPs
- **AUM**: $13,446,388,872 as of 2026-06-17

## What they sell, to whom

Wealth management for HNW/UHNW families. "Why-powered" framing (purpose-driven planning). Service line at /services-wealth. **Two distinct business lines**:
1. Wealth management for end-clients
2. **M&A for RIAs** — acquisition of other independent advisors

Both are real public-facing offerings. Pricing not disclosed (per Mather's claim of "transparent pricing"), but as a HNW/UHNW firm, expect 0.50%-1.0% AUM tiered with breakpoints.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 3 ($13.4B AUM is national-scale, media recognition real), Segment overlap 1 (HNW/UHNW, doesn't overlap USWM's early-adult or 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segments), Momentum 3 (M&A acquirer + 186 employees + Barron's recognition = high momentum, but trajectory is upmarket from USWM), Capital backing 3 (PE-backed roll-up) → **8 → low** for our segments specifically; would be **medium-high to high** if USWM's segments were HNW.

## What they're doing well

- **Two business lines, both prominent.** Wealth management + M&A for RIAs in the same nav. Most roll-ups bury their acquisition pitch; Mather makes it co-equal with their consumer offering.
- **"Why-powered wealth management"** as the differentiator. Mathers' alternative to "comprehensive" or "holistic" — leans on purpose framing.
- **Media recognition surfaced prominently.** Five publications named in the homepage hero (Top 100 RIA firms · Barron's · Top RIAs in America · Financial Advisor RIA Leaders · Financial Planning). Roll-ups use third-party validation as the primary trust signal.
- **Brand rebrand from "Sebold" to "Mather"** completed cleanly — site, ADV, brand all aligned. Confirms a deliberate roll-up brand consolidation.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **Large-firm complexity.** 186 employees means coordination overhead; USWM's "two professionals, one firm" promise becomes harder to compete with.
- **No personality at the front.** Mather doesn't lead with a named founder or principal — they lead with media-recognition logos. Effective for credibility, weak for emotional connection.
- **Roll-up brand consistency risk.** When the firm acquires another advisor practice, the named partners typically rebrand under Mather. Some attrition expected.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Mather is an exit-option benchmark, not a competitor.** If USWM grows to $200M+ AUM and considers a sale, Mather (or Mercer or Focus) is one of the buyers. Worth knowing what their public pitch looks like.
2. **"Why-powered" is an interesting alternative to "comprehensive."** USWM's voice already rejects "comprehensive" (per brand_voice avoid-list). Mather's "Why-powered" suggests there are still credible alternatives to the cliché — find USWM's equivalent (the three convictions are the answer: "trend is your friend / risk first / we'll tell you if not a fit").
3. **Media-recognition logos as trust signals.** Currently USWM has the 5.0 Google rating + 27 reviews as the main social proof. Adding "as featured in" logos as the catalog grows (even small-publication wins) follows the Mather pattern at our scale.

The narrowest single takeaway: **Mather is the largest USWM exit option** — keep tabs on their acquisition behavior (which RIA sizes / regions they're buying) as USWM grows.
