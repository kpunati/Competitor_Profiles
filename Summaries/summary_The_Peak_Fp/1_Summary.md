# Peak Financial Planning — Summary

> Woodland Hills (LA) fee-only fiduciary RIA founded **2022** by **Eric Amzalag (CFP®, RICP®)** — **$93M AUM**, 4 advisors, 7 employees, 0 DRPs. **Single-segment ICP commitment**: people in the *"Retirement Risk Zone"* with $1M+ in retirement savings. Delivers via a **published flat-fee 4-month planning engagement** ($4,000 upfront + $1,000/mo) that optionally converts to AUM wealth management with a **published 4-tier band** (1.00% under $1M → 0.50% above $10M). Eric runs a **YouTube channel with ~50,000 subscribers** as the audience-building lever.

## The headline

Peak Financial Planning is **the cleanest fee-transparent execution in our entire 20+ catalog** for an actual retail RIA — not just a tax firm or a SaaS product. They publish *both* the flat-fee planning price *and* the AUM wealth-management tier table, with explicit language that planning clients are NOT required to engage in wealth management ("Financial planning clients are NOT required to engage our firm in a wealth management relationship"). The breakdown of *what happens if you do convert* is also published step-by-step: "if at the end of month 3 a wealth management relationship is formed, the remaining $1,000 installment is cancelled and replaced with an AUM fee." That's the kind of transition mechanic most RIAs hide.

The single-segment commitment ("Retirement Risk Zone, $1M+ savings") is the most disciplined ICP move in the catalog. And the founder-led YouTube channel at ~50K subscribers is doing real audience-building work — same playbook as [Streamline_Planning](../Streamline_Planning/1_Summary.md) (Dave Zoller YouTube) but with substantially better fee-transparency execution. Threat to our retail RIA serving early-adults and mid-career 401(k) holders is **low** — different segment, different decision tree — but this is **the firm in our catalog whose pricing-page wireframe we should mirror most directly** when building our own.

## What they sell, to whom

**Two distinct services, both with published prices**:

### Service 1: Financial Planning (flat-fee, 4 months)
- **$4,000 upfront + $1,000/month × 4 months = $8,000 baseline**
- Homepage references a **$5,000–$12,000 range** across complexity tiers
- "Fiduciary on retainer for 4 months"
- Includes signature framework "Navigating the Retirement Risk Zone"
- Clients are explicitly NOT required to convert to wealth management

### Service 2: Wealth Management (AUM-based, ongoing — optional)
| Assets | Annual Fee |
|---|---|
| $0 – $999,999.99 | **1.00%** |
| $1M – $1,999,999.99 | **0.85%** |
| $2M – $10,000,000 | **0.75%** |
| Above $10M | **0.50%** |

If a planning client converts during the 4-month engagement, the remaining planning installment is halted and replaced with the AUM fee.

**ICP**: "You have over $1,000,000 in retirement savings" — explicit on the homepage. The wealth-management product's 1.00%-under-$1M band exists, but the firm's ICP signals it's not the target tier; the firm is built for the $1M–$10M household.

**Conversion path**: 3-step published funnel: Schedule Free Call → Think It Over → Have a Fiduciary in Your Corner. Plus a free webinar lead magnet ("The 4 Step System to Living Your Dream Retirement") and a public sample financial plan link.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Four-axis rubric: **Reach 2** (~50K YouTube subscribers, "In The Press" deck, podcast — real but small-creator-scale), **Segment overlap 1** (Retirement Risk Zone is squarely above our 40-50 segment by ~10–15 years), **Momentum 2** (founded only 2022 → $93M AUM in ~3 years is actually quite strong growth velocity), **Capital backing 1** (founder-led, no external capital) → sum **6 → low**.

The growth velocity is the most interesting data point: a 2022-founded firm at $93M AUM 4 years later means they've been accreting roughly **$20–25M/yr** in net new assets — driven primarily by Eric's YouTube channel and the published fee schedule. That's a useful comp for how a content-led, fee-transparent retail RIA scales in the modern era.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Best fee-transparency execution in our catalog** for a retail RIA. Published flat-fee planning + published AUM tier table + published conversion mechanic + explicit "you are NOT required to convert" framing. Strictly better than [Intrepid_Wealth_Partners](../Intrepid_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) (which only publishes the AUM table; same fee across tiers). Strictly better than [Foundry_Financial](../Foundry_Financial/1_Summary.md) (asserts transparency, gates the schedule).
- **Single-segment ICP commitment** to "Retirement Risk Zone with $1M+ retirement savings." Specifies the segment by *life event* (the decade before/after retirement) rather than by *demographic*. Compare to KDA Inc.'s six industry pages or Intrepid's six-segment list — Peak's commitment is tighter.
- **YouTube-channel-as-audience-builder at ~50K subscribers** for a firm with 4 advisors. The unit economics here are unusual: the channel produces qualified leads directly and converts to $8K+ planning engagements without an outbound sales motion.
- **Published "Form ADV" link in the footer.** Walking the talk on transparency.
- **A "sample financial plan" link** that lets prospects see what they'd actually receive before paying. Most RIAs hide deliverables behind the sales call.
- **Personality-led without being personality-only.** Eric's bio mentions hyrox mixed-doubles races and twin boys — humanizes the founder — but Joseph Perez gets equal billing as Head Planner, so the firm doesn't collapse if Eric steps back.
- **Education-first language**: "rigorous education and modeling," "via rigorous education and modeling, you can come to understand your retirement spending limits" — frames the value as *helping you make decisions* rather than *making decisions for you*. Strong fiduciary tone.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **No early-adult / 401(k) surface at all.** ICP is explicitly $1M+ retirees. Our two target segments are invisible to this firm — which makes them low-threat but also useful as a *what-good-looks-like* reference.
- **State/SEC registration ambiguity.** Footer says "State of CA"; IAPD shows SEC 801- file number. Either an outdated footer copy or an active transition. For a firm whose pitch is transparency, the unresolved framing is a small wart.
- **Key-person risk on Eric.** The YouTube channel is "Eric Amzalag" not "Peak Financial Planning." If Eric leaves or steps back, the lead engine stops.
- **Geographic concentration** (single Woodland Hills office). National virtual delivery mitigates this but the trust-anchor is LA.
- **Site footer "© 2024 Peak Financial Planning"** is outdated (currently 2026). Same small-firm-execution wart as KDA Inc.'s inconsistent review counts.

## What this means for our entry

Peak FP is **not on our threat radar for direct client overlap**. But it's the **single most useful reference firm** in our catalog for the question "what does a fee-transparent, content-led, single-segment retail RIA look like at $93M AUM, 3 years post-launch?"

1. **Mirror the pricing page directly.** Two-service structure (planning + wealth management). Published prices for both. Explicit "planning clients are NOT required to convert." Published conversion mechanic. This is the wireframe.
2. **Steal the "education-first" tonal commitment.** "Via rigorous education and modeling, you can come to understand your retirement spending limits and make your own choices" — this voice positions the firm as *enabling client decisions* rather than *making them.* The right register for our two segments (early-adults learning to invest for the first time, mid-career 401(k) holders optimizing).
3. **Single-segment commitment is the right discipline** — but be honest about the trade-offs. Peak's $1M+ retirement-savings ICP means their TAM is much smaller than ours would be; for our early-adult and 40-50 segments combined, we can credibly serve a larger audience without diluting if we commit *segment-by-segment* on dedicated landing pages (the KDA Inc. industry-pages pattern).
4. **The 50K YouTube + free webinar + sample financial plan + Form ADV download** trio is a powerful trust-and-acquisition stack that costs very little to operate after the initial production investment.
5. **Velocity benchmark**: ~$20–25M AUM/yr net inflow for a 2022-founded, content-led, fee-transparent retail RIA in a defined segment. That's what we should plan against — *not* the Focus Partners / Merit Financial trajectory, which is M&A-driven and PE-backed.

What we should explicitly *not* copy: the no-early-adult / no-401(k) surface (we need both), the single-geography concentration, and the unresolved State-of-CA vs SEC-801 framing (resolve before launch).

The narrowest single takeaway: **Peak Financial Planning's pricing page is the firm in our catalog whose pricing-page wireframe we should mirror most directly.** Combined with KDA Inc.'s segment-page architecture and Tax Alchemy's "tax savings guarantee" close mechanic, the playbook for our public-facing pricing surface is now well-mapped.
