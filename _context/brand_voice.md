## 3. WHAT WE BELIEVE (THE THREE CONVICTIONS)

These three convictions are the most distinctive content in our brand and they appear in nearly every piece of long-form material we produce.

### 01. The trend is your friend.
We follow what the market is already doing — we don't claim to predict where it's going next. Discipline beats intuition.

### 02. Risk first. Returns second.
Capital preservation drives every position. Most clients we meet are over-positioned for growth and under-positioned for downside.

### 03. If we're not a fit, we'll tell you.
Bad-fit clients hurt everyone. We'd rather turn away a prospect than charge them for years of service that doesn't match what they need.

### Why these matter
Nearly every wealth management firm talks about values. We don't — we talk about convictions, and we attach specific behavior to each one. This is one of the most distinctive things in our marketing language. Always lean on these when there's a chance to.

## 4. INVESTMENT PHILOSOPHY (THE TECHNICAL VOCABULARY)

We talk about HOW we invest using a specific set of terms. These are the words that appear in our investment management positioning:

- **Trend following** — primary methodology
- **Rules-based** — disciplined, systematic, not emotional
- **Momentum** — riding established price movements
- **Options overlay** — using options to reduce risk and enhance yield
- **Position sizing** — calibrating exposure to individual positions based on risk
- **Risk management** — capital preservation as the core priority
- **Active management** — deliberate, hands-on portfolio decisions
- **Capital preservation** — protecting downside before chasing upside
- **Fee-only** — paid by clients only, no commissions
- **Fiduciary** — legally obligated to act in client's best interest

These terms should appear naturally in any investment-related content. They're not jargon for jargon's sake — they're the actual methodology.

## 5. VOICE & TONE

### How we sound
- **Confident, not boastful.** We state things directly. We don't say "industry-leading" or "best-in-class" or "world-class."
- **Honest broker.** We mention when we walk away from work. We acknowledge tradeoffs. We don't oversell.
- **Editorial, not corporate.** Closer to a Sunday business magazine (Bloomberg, Barron's) than a corporate brochure.
- **Specific over generic.** We use real names, real credentials, real claims. Never "our team of experts" or "we have decades of experience."
- **Quiet authority.** Like a senior partner in conversation, not a salesperson on a stage.

### Signature stylistic moves

**Gold-italic emphasis on standout phrases.** This is our most distinctive typographic move. Inside otherwise straight copy, one phrase gets gold-italic treatment.

Examples:
- "A small firm. ***Real conviction***."
- "Real work. ***Done well***."
- "Active investment management. ***Built around your life***."
- "Let's see if we're ***a fit***."
- "We tell prospective clients ***honestly*** when we're not the right fit."

**Short declarative sentences alternated with longer ones.** We avoid wall-of-text paragraphs. Rhythm matters.

**Direct address to "you" the reader.** Not "our clients" — "you."

## 9. CONTENT RULES

### When writing about services
- Lead with **Investment Management** — it's the primary offering
- Tax Planning, Trust & Estate, Retirement Planning, College Planning, Insurance, Cash Flow, Employee Benefits, Business Owner Services — these are supporting services
- For business owners, mention Outsourced Tax / Accounting / Bookkeeping / Payroll bundle
- Always close with a CTA pointing to /contact-us/

### When writing about the firm
- Lead with conviction over feature lists
- Reference specific people (Kean, Beata) when relevant — not "our team"
- Mention Chatham, NJ when relevant — it's a credibility marker
- Use the three convictions when there's a chance

### When writing CTAs
- Vary the language: "Schedule a Consultation" / "Book Your Consultation" / "Let's Talk" / "Speak with Our Team" — same destination (/contact-us/), different phrasing
- Don't repeat the same CTA phrase three times in one piece
- "30-minute consultation. No pitch, no pressure." is the standard description

### When writing about Kean
- Lead with credentials: "MS Finance, CFA"
- Reference career: Merrill Lynch, JPMorgan Chase, Citi, UBS, AIG
- Focus on investment management, options strategies, risk mitigation

### When writing about Beata
- Lead with credentials: "EA, NATP"
- Focus on tax strategy, business advisory, IRS representation
- She's the secondary face but never minimized
