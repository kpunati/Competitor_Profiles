# Patterns to Borrow

*A curated, action-oriented extract of the catalog's highest-leverage tactical patterns. The `What_The_Market_Looks_Like.md` doc analyzes patterns with strength/weakness tradeoffs; this file is its drafting-oriented complement — fast lookup for "we want to do something like X — who already does this well?"*

*Synthesized 2026-06-17 from 48 competitor profiles.*

---

## Pricing structure patterns

- **Three productized tiers with AUM thresholds** — [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) — Start Up (<$1M) / Core ($1M-$10M) / Private ($10M+). Each tier has explicit bundled-vs-add-on services. The cleanest tier productization in the catalog. *USWM borrow: Foundation / Growth / Wealth at parallel AUM thresholds.*
- **Year-1 vs. renewal pricing** — [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) — Essential $3,900/$2,900 · Strategic $5,200/$4,200 · Comprehensive $9,000/$8,000. Heavier Year-1 reflects initial plan-build effort; lower renewal softens the renewal decision psychologically. *USWM borrow: same structure across any tier.*
- **Published `/fees/` page as a top-level nav item** — [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) — `/why-pure/fees/`. $11B firm publishing fees is a transparency signal. *USWM borrow: at launch, even rough tiers beat the gated-fee status most peers default to.*
- **"No AUM fees—ever"** as the differentiator vs. traditional RIAs — [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md). Reframes the AUM-fee question as a competitive choice, not a default. *USWM borrow: not the same posture (USWM keeps AUM for managed assets) but the framing is borrowable for the flat-fee entry tier.*
- **Subscription as the actual product, not a footnote** — [Abundo Wealth](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($0 AUM by design, 12 advisors), [Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) ($5K flat-fee Plan). *USWM borrow: the entry-tier Foundation Plan can be priced as a standalone product.*

## Homepage hero patterns

- **Three named ICP personas on the homepage** — [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) — John & Laura ("Ready for Retirement") · Amy & Dave ("Work Optional") · Carolyn ("Single & Meaningful"). Each persona names: relationship status, timeline, specific concerns, specific priorities. *USWM borrow: two persona triples per target segment (one for early-adults, one for 40-50 mid-career).*
- **Explicit 4-vs-4 anti-wirehouse contrast table** — [Truepoint Wealth Counsel](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md) — *"People · Compassion · Your Trust · Return on Life"* vs. *"Products · Commissions · Our Bottom Line · Return on Investment."* The catalog's strongest copywriting. *USWM borrow: a sharper version with Kean's wirehouse-defection arc as the credibility anchor.*
- **Stats trifecta on the homepage** — [Modera Wealth Management](../Competitors/Modera_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md) — "$17.5B / 11.2 / 6,300+" (AUM / avg retention years / client count). Concrete numbers as the hero. *USWM borrow: once metrics exist (founding year / client count / case-study count), surface them at the same prominence.*
- **"With BKFi" vs. "Without BKFi" comparison** — [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md). Side-by-side framing of "the alternative" vs. "us." *USWM borrow: a version that contrasts active-management-without-tax-integration vs. USWM.*

## Voice & narrative patterns

- **Generational niche label** — [Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) — "HENRYs" (High Earner, Not Rich Yet). Audience self-identifies. *USWM borrow: names for the two target segments (e.g., "First-Account Founders" for early-adults · "401(k) Veterans" for 40-50 cohort). Test against real prospects before committing.*
- **Geographic specificity by named employers** — [Cordant Wealth Partners](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) — Intel / Microsoft / Nike / Amazon / Apple. Prospect self-identifies immediately. *USWM borrow: name NJ-area employers (Merck, J&J, Verizon, Prudential, RWJBarnabas, BMS) for the 401(k) segment.*
- **"Return on Life" as a measurement reframe** — [Truepoint](../Competitors/Truepoint_Wealth_Counsel/1_Summary.md). Subordinates investment returns to life outcomes. *USWM borrow: aligns with the existing "Active investment management. Built around your life." tagline.*
- **Mission framing (activist language)** — [Abundo Wealth](../Competitors/Abundo_Wealth/1_Summary.md) — "We're on a mission to change the financial planning industry." Unusual register for an advisory firm; gives recruiting + brand-conviction advantages. *USWM borrow: the three convictions ARE a mission — surface them as such.*
- **"Live freely" as a 2-word promise** — [Compass Ion Advisors](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md). Tagline doubles as the site headline and the closing CTA. Consistent through 5 pages. *USWM borrow: "Built around your life" or "Real conviction" can function the same way.*

## Funnel patterns

- **Free education event as top-of-funnel** — [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) — "Attend a Retirement Class" alongside "Talk to an Advisor." Lower-stakes entry captures email + qualified-lead intent before the consultation conversation. *USWM borrow: "First Investment Account Class" (early-adults) or "401(k) Rollover Class" (40-50 cohort).*
- **Ebook lead magnet** — [Cordant](../Competitors/Cordant_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) — "GRAB OUR LATEST EBOOK." Soft entry that builds the email list. *USWM borrow: an ebook per target segment ("The First-Investor Field Guide" / "The Mid-Career 401(k) Playbook").*
- **"Match With An Advisor" questionnaire** — [Avise Financial](../Competitors/Avise_Financial/1_Summary.md). Three-step intake before the live conversation. *USWM borrow: only if the firm scales to multi-advisor coverage; not at launch with the two-person team.*
- **Trust stack ("no minimums / cancel anytime / 100% refund")** — [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md). Aggressive risk-reversal as the trust signal. *USWM borrow: the flat-fee entry product can carry a satisfaction-or-refund commitment.*

## Service-line patterns

- **401(k) consulting as a co-equal homepage pillar** — [Greenbush Financial Group](../Competitors/Greenbush_Financial_Group/1_Summary.md). The only firm in the 48-firm catalog to do this. *USWM borrow: this is the direct 40-50 segment opening per `Where_We_Should_Play.md` → Opening 2. Make it a named pillar.*
- **In-house tax bench** — [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) — 5 named CPAs led by a CPA Director (Dan Beyer). Tax filing bundled into wealth management, not referred out. *USWM borrow: Beata's EA + NATP is the equivalent at our scale. Promote with same prominence.*
- **Named content franchise** (vs. generic "Blog") — [Compass Ion](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("Guide for Good"), [Pure Financial](../Competitors/Pure_Financial_Advisors/1_Summary.md) ("Your Money Your Wealth" podcast), [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) ("Nick's Weekly Memo"). *USWM borrow: a named content surface ("The Trend Memo" / "Real Conviction" / "Notes from the Other Side of Wall Street") instead of "Blog."*
- **Multi-credential founder pairing** — [Brooklyn FI](../Competitors/Brooklyn_Fi/1_Summary.md) — AJ Ayers (CFP/EA/CEP) + Shane Mason (CPA/PFS/CFP/CPWA). Complementary credential stacks cover blind spots. *USWM borrow: Kean (MS Finance, CFA) + Beata (EA, NATP) is structurally the same shape — surface the pairing publicly.*
- **Investment Committee as governance pattern** — [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) — 3 outside CPAs (Prusa, Webster, VanDerSchaaf) sit on an investment committee. Separates allocation decisions from client-relationship work, gives prospects confidence. *USWM borrow: optional structural pattern as the firm grows past the founder-led stage.*

## Founder-story patterns

- **Prior-career-to-CFP arc with emotional authenticity** — [Brindle & Bay](../Competitors/Brindle_And_Bay/1_Summary.md) — Nick Davis was a full-time pastor for 10 years before financial planning. "Golden Rule as operating principle" works because the source is authentic. *USWM borrow: Kean's wirehouse arc (20+ years at Merrill, JPMorgan Chase, Citi, UBS, AIG) is the equivalent — a prior-vocation credibility story.*
- **Anti-corporate departure arc** — [Stash Wealth](../Competitors/Stash_Wealth/1_Summary.md) — Priya Malani went from professional ballet → Merrill Lynch HNW → founder. Origin story rooted in observing her parents' financial stress. *USWM borrow: Kean's "I worked at the big firms. I left to do it right" story should be on the About page.*
- **Multi-exit operator pedigree** — [Domain Money](../Competitors/Domain_Money/1_Summary.md) — Adam Dell: Clarity Money (sold to Goldman Sachs, led Marcus product), MessageOne (sold to Dell), Buzzsaw (sold to Autodesk). Credibility shortcut. *USWM borrow: Kean's named-firm trajectory (Merrill / JPMorgan / Citi / UBS / AIG) is the wirehouse-side analog.*

## Anti-patterns (what NOT to borrow even when they look attractive)

- **"Our team of experts" generic team-page bios** — used by almost every firm in the catalog. Per `_context/brand_what_not_to_make.md` § 3 — replace with editorial profile treatment + named credentials.
- **"Comprehensive holistic wealth management"** — used by [Plancorp](../Competitors/Plancorp/1_Summary.md) on their homepage hero. Both words on USWM's avoid-list.
- **4-step "Discovery → Plan → Implement → Review" process diagram** — used by 30+ firms in the catalog. Filler. Per `brand_what_not_to_make.md` § 3.
- **Multi-niche menu strategy** — [Wiser Wealth Management](../Competitors/Wiser_Wealth_Management/1_Summary.md)'s 6-niche pattern (Retirement / Business Owners / Single Again / Airline Pilots / Legacy / Inheritance) dilutes segment commitment. USWM's two-segment focus is structurally cleaner.
- **WordPress default meta description** — [Compass Ion](../Competitors/Compass_Ion_Advisors/1_Summary.md) shipped their site with "This is an example page... I'm a bike messenger by day..." still in the meta. Pre-launch checklist: deliberate meta + OG image pass.
