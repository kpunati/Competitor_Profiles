# Our Thesis

*The standing context every competitor profile is written against. This is what we know about our intended market entry — rough is fine, sharpens over time.*

*Last updated: 2026-05-11*

---

## Who we want to serve

We're targeting multiple demographics deliberately, and want to be able to address each one with its own messaging and triggers — not collapse them into a single generic pitch.

Initial segments:

- **Early adults setting up proper investment accounts** — the first-time-investor cohort. Triggers: starting a career, first real income, "I should be doing this but don't know how," choice paralysis between robo-advisors and DIY brokerages.
- **40–50 year olds focused on 401(k) management** — mid-career, accumulated some balance, anxious about retirement readiness, often have a 401(k) at work plus held-away assets that aren't being managed together.
- **(More segments to be added as we sharpen the strategy.)**

The competitive analysis should help us understand, for each segment: who is currently serving them well, who is underserving them, and what messaging triggers actually work.

## What edge we think we have

- **Experienced independent firm.** Established business owner with a strong background — not a roboadvisor, not a green new entrant. Independent (not tied to a broker-dealer's product shelf), which is differentiating against the wirehouses and bank-owned advisors.
- **Modern data and tech tooling.** With current team additions, we'll be operating with top-tier data, information, and tech tools — meaningfully more modern than the typical independent RIA, while keeping the human-advisor relationship that pure-fintech players can't match.

The implied positioning we should pressure-test against competitors: *the independent advisor who actually uses modern tools*. Lots of competitors will claim one of those two halves; few will credibly claim both.

## What's off the table

Nothing hard-ruled-out at this stage.

---

## How the analyzer should use this

For every competitor profile:

1. **Who do they serve, and how well do they serve our target segments specifically?** Don't just describe their ICP — score it against ours.
2. **Where does their positioning land vs. our intended edge** (independent + modern)? Are they pure-tech (vulnerable to "no real advisor"), pure-traditional (vulnerable to "stuck in the 90s"), or actually credible on both?
3. **What triggers and messages do they use for each demographic** we care about? Pull direct quotes — these are the building blocks for our own copy.
4. **What gap does this competitor leave open** that someone with our edge could credibly fill?
