# Advize Wealth Management — Summary

> A one-person, fee-only RIA in Rochester, Michigan, built around founder Colin Overweg, CFP®, who left the broker-dealer world to sell "modern financial planning" — independent, fee-only, advice-only — primarily to busy mid-career professionals.

## The headline

Advize is the closest single-person version of the exact positioning our thesis claims: *"independent, fee-only financial planning firm powered by the industries leading technology."* It's the same two halves — independent + modern tech — sold by one CFP® with a Wix-built website, a Mailchimp newsletter ("Colin's Column"), and a Zoom-scheduled intro funnel. Colin is a credentialed solo operator (CFP®, FPA board, claims "Investopedia Top 100 Advisor"); he is not a competitor for scale or coverage. He is the proof-of-concept that our two-halves positioning *is* marketable — and a clean demonstration of the gap a multi-advisor firm with real tooling can credibly fill.

## What they sell, to whom

A single packaged service: comprehensive, fee-only, advice-only financial planning — the firm's tagline is *"No products, just advice!"* The named ICP is *"busy professionals achieving financial freedom"* (About page) and the StartHere page narrows further to *"Ready to retire on your terms?!"* — i.e. mid-career-and-later professionals planning their way to retirement. Pricing is gated behind a video on the StartHere funnel (watch a 5-min fees & services video → schedule a free Zoom intro at `scheduler.zoom.us/advize/intro`), so fees aren't on the public site but Colin shows them on camera before any call. No tiers, no self-serve signup, no published minimum.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Advize is a solo-advisor practice with no visible team, a Wix website, and no segment-specific funnels. He is not competing for the same volume of prospects a multi-advisor firm needs — and he likely cannot, given the one-person capacity ceiling (~50–80 quality relationships). His content engine (videos, FPA board, Investopedia mention, "Colin's Column" newsletter) gives him expert-authority reach that punches above his AUM weight, so he will show up in Google searches for things like *"fee-only advisor Michigan"* and *"modern financial planning."* But where our segments are concerned — early-adult first-time investors and 40–50 401(k) holders — Advize addresses neither operationally: the early-adult segment is invisible, and the 401(k) segment is targeted thematically but with no 401(k)-specific surface, content, or funnel.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **A specific, named villain in the founding story.** *"I was sick of commissions, proprietary products, sales quotes, clucky technology and being told how to help my clients"* — vivid, concrete, and far better than the generic "fee-only" line most independents lean on. It makes *"No products, just advice!"* land.
- **A two-step "StartHere" funnel** (watch a fees-and-services video → book a Zoom intro). Qualifies prospects, surfaces fees pre-call, and is a creator-economy pattern translated cleanly into wealth management.
- **First-person founder voice.** *"Hi, I'm Colin."* Rare in this category. Trust-building for prospects who want to know exactly who they're talking to.
- **A stacked credentials wall.** CFP® + FPA board seat + Investopedia Top 100 + university guest lectures + named media mentions. Each is common; the stack is uncommon.
- **A named newsletter with character.** "Colin's Column" is footer-promoted on every page — a long-compounded email list is a more durable asset than any individual blog post.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **The "modern technology" claim is asserted, not shown.** The site is a Wix template with no client-portal screenshot, no planning-software preview, no dashboard demo. A prospect doing real diligence will notice — and a firm that *shows* the tech wins on the same positioning Colin only claims.
- **No early-adult surface at all.** Every visible content surface assumes a prospect with assets and a retirement horizon already in view. A 28-year-old opening their first Roth IRA will feel addressed nowhere on this site.
- **No 401(k)-specific funnel** despite the segment fitting Colin's stated ICP. No rollover content, no held-away-401(k) management surface, no employer-plan-coordination language.
- **No "fiduciary" claim in captured copy** — for a fee-only RIA, that's a missing trust word a competitor can pre-empt.
- **No testimonials.** SEC's 2021 marketing rule allows them with proper disclosure; Advize is leaving free trust signals on the table.
- **Voice fragility** — visible typos (*"clucky technology"*, *"have been feature in multiple publications"*) and a desktop homepage that renders almost empty below the fold. Tolerable from a one-person practice; not tolerable from a firm presenting itself as a serious organization.

## What this means for our entry

**Advize is the proof that our positioning works as a pitch — and the demonstration of the gap our team can credibly fill.** Colin has shown that "independent + modern tech" is a sellable claim in this category. The opening he leaves wide open is two-fold: (1) **show the modern tech instead of just asserting it** (publish the planning-software stack, screenshot the client portal, demo the dashboard); and (2) **segment from the homepage** rather than running early-adults and mid-career-401(k) holders through the same "busy professionals" funnel. Don't try to out-content Colin — he has a 5+ year head start on his newsletter and YouTube presence. Out-show him on the tech, and out-specific him on the segments.
