**Overview**

Colin Overweg is the founder of Advize Wealth, an Investopedia Top 100 Advisor, and member of the Board for the Financial Planning Association. He’s also been featured in publications like Barron’s, CNBC and The New York Times and a frequent lecturer at various universities.

In this episode, we talk about how building wealth today is radically different than it used to be, pensions, misconceptions about home ownership, how to get started with investing, whole life insurance, Nancy Pelosi’s portfolio and more.

Please enjoy our conversation with Colin Overweg.

**Key Takeaways**

1.  Building wealth today is significantly different than in previous generations, especially due to the disappearance of pensions and decreased equity compensation options in companies.
    
2.  Younger generation often faces confusion and misconceptions about investment strategies, such as the notion of needing to be wealthy to start investing or the overemphasis on home ownership as a wealth-building decision.
    
3.  Low-cost index funds, which allow investors to own shares in multiple companies, are recommended for long-term investment. These have shown to outperform around 90% of professional hedge fund managers and traders.
    
4.  The trend in the financial industry is leaning towards more DIY investing, facilitated by FinTech tools. However, seeking advice from financial advisors still remains relevant.
