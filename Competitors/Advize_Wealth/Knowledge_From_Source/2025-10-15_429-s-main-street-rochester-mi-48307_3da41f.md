## **The Hidden Risks of Being Too Conservative**

### Inflation Risk

Investing too conservatively, like [holding primarily cash and bonds](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/investing/103015/cash-vs-bonds-what-pick-times-uncertainty.asp), leaves your portfolio trailing behind rising costs as inflation steadily reduces how far your retirement income will stretch. Over decades, cash and bonds often fail to keep pace with inflation. 

"The real risk isn't short-term market swings, it's running out of money in your 80s or 90s because your portfolio didn't keep pace with inflation," said Overweg.

Overweg also said that he recommends allocations based on his client's needs: "Every plan is unique, but a reasonable framework is to keep one to two years of expected income withdrawals in cash, an additional five years of withdrawals in bonds for stability, and the rest in a [globally diversified stock portfolio](https://www.investopedia.com/international-stocks-lower-portfolio-risk-11759959). This gives your stocks at least an eight-year timeline, which really starts to put the odds of success in your favor."
