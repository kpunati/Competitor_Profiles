# Cash Back Credit Cards Are Great, Too

### **The Downside of Points Strategies**

While many people have fun maximizing points-based credit cards and redeeming points for exciting aspirational travel opportunities, the reality is that it can now feel like a part-time job.

What was once a niche hobby has exploded into a big industry. And as more and more people collect more and more points, there simply aren’t enough cheap business class seats to go around. Airlines have figured this out, too, and have steadily been decreasing the availability of cheap inventory while simultaneously requiring more points to redeem the same flights.

If you are like most people, you just want to pick a vacation spot you like and go there on a convenient flight during whatever week of the year works best for your schedule. That (totally normal) style of vacation planning just doesn’t match up well at all with the new points game which often demands stalking limited redemption opportunities for weeks or months, accepting last minute flights, switching destinations, choosing a new time, and/or taking inconvenient routes. And that’s before accounting for the inconvenience of applying for and juggling a lot of credit cards.

You can try this for yourself: pick a specific international destination and specific dates you want to go there. Then try to find cheap business class ticket deals with points. You will likely be disappointed by what you find.

### **The Role of Cash Back**

For the last decade or so, cash back strategies have gotten something of a bad rap. Because the value of the rewards is fixed, there are no opportunities for skilled redeemers to earn outsized value.

But with the increasing complexity of points strategies noted above, the pendulum is swinging back to simplicity and flexibility. And cash back provides that every day of the week. In fact, with just 3-4 well-chosen cards, it’s very realistic to earn ~3% back on average across all your spending with minimal effort.

The key, as it so often is, is building a simple system that you can stick with.

### **A Framework for Cash Back**

There are three major “team members” in any successful cash back credit card strategy which can be mapped directly to your personal spending habits.

**Team Member 1 - The “Catch-All” Card:** This is the foundation of the entire strategy. A catch-all card earns a flat rate - usually 2% - on any purchase no matter the category. No thinking, no effort. If you did nothing else but use this card for all spending, you’d already be ahead of most people.

**Examples:** Fidelity Visa, Citi DoubleCash, Capital One Venture

**Team Member 2 - The Major Category Specialist:** For many households, food, gas, and travel are major annual expense categories. It is common to spend thousands of dollars per year on them. They also happen to be categories where cash back credit cards offer more generous bonus rewards because the card companies want to capture that spending.

Note: Because there’s no single perfect card for all major categories, most people end up with two Major Category Specialists for better coverage.

It’s common to find cash back rates ranging from 3%-6% on these categories, depending on the card. You do have to remember to use that card for that category, but with only a couple cards that tends to be manageable. And which specific card you get should be based on the categories you actually spend the most on.

One little “gotcha” to watch out for when creating your plan: some cards place annual limits on how much spending can qualify for the higher rate. For example, the Amex Blue Cash Preferred offers 6% back on groceries, but only on your first $6,000 of annual grocery spending. So if you spend more than $500 per month on groceries, you would either need another card for that extra spend or switch to your “Catch-All Card” and take the 2%.

**Examples:** Citi CustomCash, Chase Freedom Flex, Amex Blue Cash Preferred, Capital One Savor

**Team Member 3 - The Store Specialist:** Okay, we all have our store where we shop way too often. We see it on the credit card statements. It’s usually something like Target / Target / Target / Target or Amazon / Amazon / Amazon / Amazon. If you have a single store like that where thousands of dollars of spending is dedicated every year, it may be worth getting that store’s specific cash back card. Target and Amazon (which includes Whole Foods) are two examples of retailer-specific cards that offer 5% back on all purchases. That’s a tough earning rate to beat for a significant portion of your spending.

Examples: Target Card, Amazon Card, Kroger Mastercard, REI Mastercard

### **Putting the Team Together**

  
Imagine someone who with the following spending pattern:

-   $3,250 per month on general purchases
-   $500 per month on groceries
-   $500 per month on travel
-   $1,000 per month at Amazon / Whole Foods.

  
That person could create the following 4 card team:

-   Citi DoubleCash (2% on everything)
-   Amex Blue Cash Preferred (6% on groceries up to $6K per year)
-   Citi Custom Cash (5% on a single category up to $6K per year, in this case travel)
-   Amazon Visa card (5% on Amazon)

They would earn roughly $170 in cash back rewards every month or a little over $2,000 per year. On $5,000 of monthly spending, that is a 3.2% effective cash back rate.

Not bad for such minimal work! Note this is just one example, and the right cards for your situation will vary.

### **Practical Tips for Implementation**

Start with the catch-all card. If you only add one card, make it this one. It immediately improves every purchase. Then add one specialist card at a time. Don’t overhaul your wallet overnight. Layer in a grocery & gas card, for example, and get accustomed to remembering to use that card for those purchases. Then add the niche retailer card to round out the system.

If you have trouble keeping track of which cards to use where, you could try sticking a little label on one of the cards (e.g. to label your grocery and gas card). And if you shop a lot online, for example at Amazon, it is extra easy to remember because all you have to do is load that card into your digital wallet one time and then it will become the default.

### **Conclusion**

You don’t need a complex, points-heavy strategy to get strong value from credit cards. A simple, well-structured cash back setup can quietly generate thousands of dollars per year with very little effort. And more importantly, it does so in a way that fits seamlessly into your life.

Because just like investing, the best system is the one you’ll actually stick with.
