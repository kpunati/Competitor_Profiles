# When Do You Need a Financial Advisor?

Throughout our lives, it’s usually clear when we should meet with professionals like doctors or dentists on a regular basis. But when we need help in other parts of our lives, like our finances, it may be harder to pinpoint. *When should you think about hiring a financial advisor?*

### **The Conventional Wisdom**

  
Traditionally, people tend to seek out a financial advisor when:

-   **They encounter a big financial change:** when there’s unexpected inheritance, a large stock equity payout, or a large pay increase.
-   **Start planning for retirement:** see if they’re on track or figure out how to transition from earning an income to spending down assets.

People tend to believe they need a financial advisor *after* they’ve built or come into wealth, but everyone can benefit from the help of a financial advisor at different stages in their lives. For example, budgeting and having a strong handle on your cash flow is especially important for those just starting out in their career and will have a huge impact.

Consider seeking a financial advisor *before* you’ve accumulated wealth to help you achieve your life goals faster and easier.

Unlike most financial advisors, the advice we provide our clients at Abundo doesn’t depend on the amount of wealth they’ve already created. Rather, we also focus on budgeting and low-cost investing strategies that will benefit people at all stages of their lives.

### **Building a Foundation Early**

Oftentimes, those with the most to gain from personalized financial advice are the ones just getting started. People in their 20s and 30s who are navigating student loan debt and beginning their careers can benefit the most with setting a solid financial foundation.

Making the right decisions in these areas can have a massive influence on how quickly you build your net worth:

-   Budgeting
-   Where best to save (401(k) vs. Roth IRA vs. HSA)
-   How to pay off debt
-   Selecting the right benefits at work
-   Taking advantage of [compound interest](https://www.investor.gov/financial-tools-calculators/calculators/compound-interest-calculator) early and often

Learning how to make good financial decisions earlier in life will build a foundation for future financial success. If you have a financial advisor providing accountability around savings habits in your 20s, it will make a huge difference in the total wealth accumulated over your lifetime:

Here’s an example of the amount of savings you’d have at age 60 when initially investing $10,000 and saving $500 each month with an 8% compound rate:

-   If you started at age 20: **$1,771,584**
-   If you started at age 30: **$780,325**
-   If you started at age 40: **$321,181**
-   If you started at age 50: **$108,508**

**Prioritizing Goals & Navigating Life Transitions**

We believe financial planning is not just about advice on a single important decision, such as how to transition to retirement or what to do with an inheritance. Rather, ongoing financial planning has the biggest long-term impact.

Consider everything that can change in a given year: marriage, having a child, changing jobs, making a large purchase, having unexpected expenses, and more. Plus, there could be changes with the stock market, taxes, or legislation that is hard to navigate how these could impact you on your own.

A good financial advisor who focuses on comprehensive financial planning will help their clients in all areas of their financial lives. Anything that relates to your ability to spend, save, or earn money is important and making the right decisions is what truly creates wealth.
