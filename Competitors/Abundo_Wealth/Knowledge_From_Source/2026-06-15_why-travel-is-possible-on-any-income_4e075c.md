# Why Travel Is Possible on Any Income

*Emmanuella Desruisseaux is a student at Rice University and an intern with Abundo Wealth. Despite growing up in a low-income household and working at a minimum wage job in college, she has been able to achieve amazing travel experiences including Quebec, Mexico, and New York. She is excited to share her wisdom on savvy traveling in her first published blog post!*  

### When Travel Feels Out of Reach

South Africa, Vietnam, France. What do these places have in common? They are all popular travel destinations that typically cost over $1,000 to travel to!

Because travel can be so expensive, many people end up choosing between three not-so-great options:

-   Travel to cheaper places, even if those places don’t fully stimulate them.
-   Incur debt to travel to the places they want to go.
-   Give up on travel altogether. 

  
What if there were a 4th option that allowed you to travel to the places you want to go without incurring debt? Despite the barriers, including high prices, this is very possible to achieve! That’s why I want to talk about what travel can look like when money is tight, and why it still matters.  

### **Growing to See Travel Differently**

I grew up in a low income household where traveling was not a priority. Many of my friends had the opposite experience. They were able to go to new countries and cities and participate in cool experiences. For me, it just wasn’t as easy, and that’s true for many people.

My main goal is to remove the stigma that just because you don’t come from a lot of money means that you can’t have a travel life. Travel is important for many people, regardless of their income. It brings you to new experiences in new settings that can lead to lowered stress levels, personal growth, and a cultural immersion that can help you learn more about and connect with yourself and others. These are all benefits of travel that I have personally experienced!

Despite not having a lot of resources, I was still able to travel to some interesting places with my family like New York, Montreal, and Haiti. These were essentially places where we knew people who lived there. The purpose was not to seek out a new and personal experience, but rather to connect with people we knew. That’s a wonderful thing to do, but I wanted more! I wanted more fun!

### **Making a Trip Work on Your Income**

My favorite trip was the one I made to the Teotihuacan Pyramids in Mexico. This was such an amazing trip for me; it’s the type of thing I saw my friends doing growing up, but I wasn’t sure if I would be able to do. Remember, I’m a college student who makes minimum wage! And I only work a few hours a week.

### **#1: Create a Positive Travel Loop With Credit Cards**

Learning about [credit cards](https://thepointsguy.com/credit-cards/short-airline-pm/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=NBDtraE1-best%20credit%20card%20for%20flights-774486812344&utm_term=best%20credit%20card%20for%20flights&utm_cmpid=21780736698&utm_adgid=172252452927&utm_tgtid=kwd-297151509014&utm_mt=b&utm_adid=774486812344&utm_dvc=c&utm_ntwk=g&utm_adpos=&utm_plcmnt=&utm_locphysid=9197666&utm_locintid=&utm_feeditemid=&utm_devicemdl=&utm_plcmnttgt=&utm_misc=&utm_ltpcid=Cj0KCQjwp7jOBhDGARIsABe7C4dfMjWAen9DuaMB6cP1oxnRs3D9diyGWJPE8QT3Z1Cvbp-k2HmH870aAjS5EALw_wcB&utm_paid=-pm&gad_source=1&gad_campaignid=21780736698&gbraid=0AAAAADKlpfqBSYD4TtWFEB9voat_1zudB&gclid=Cj0KCQjwp7jOBhDGARIsABe7C4dfMjWAen9DuaMB6cP1oxnRs3D9diyGWJPE8QT3Z1Cvbp-k2HmH870aAjS5EALw_wcB) and how people use them to fund their trips was eye-opening for me. I now think of credit cards like creating a positive travel loop. You can open a card with a reasonable minimum spend to get the bonus and then get additional points by purchasing my travel expenses on the card. That will give me enough points to cover my next trip!  
Buying the flights, paying for the hotel, and buying food/clothes when I go out in the city all contribute toward free travel in the future if you know how to work the points system. While influencers might be showing off business class cabins, you can find incredible deals on economy class airfare and inexpensive hotels that cost a fraction of the points.  
I acknowledge credit cards can be risky, though. So please make sure if you use them that you stick to your budget and only put purchases on the credit card you know you can pay off without being charged interest or having your credit negatively impacted. Pay attention to the minimum spend requirements as well. For example, if you have to spend $1,000 to get 70,000 points, then as long as you can pay off $1,000 you can limit the credit card usage to that amount. Don’t put more purchases on it if you can’t pay it off.

**#2: Travel with Partners To Save Big**

As long as you are not committed to the idea of traveling solo, it is highly beneficial to travel with someone else who shares your values. That means they have similar travel interests and budget!

Lodging is often one of the biggest costs for any trip. And having a friend to share a room with can produce 50% savings for both of you on that budget item. That means you can go on twice as many trips and have twice as many life experiences together compared to what you could have done individually. Plus, you develop deeper bonds with the people you travel with and hopefully create lasting lifelong friendships.

The benefits are not limited to lodging, either. For example, during my monthlong summer trip to Mexico, I went with some friends and we saved hundreds of dollars by splitting rideshares and food. Cab rides can actually be pretty affordable when split multiple ways, and having access to that transportation opens up more experiences  
that are further outside the city. And with food, you can try more exciting things when a few people share multiple dishes!

**#4: Be Flexible With Accommodations**

You may notice I mentioned a hostel for my Mexico trip. Remember the point of traveling for me is to see the world. So I’m willing to sacrifice a little bit on the accommodations to make that goal possible. Every goal comes with some sacrifice!  
There’s too much fun stuff to see, so I don’t want to spend all my money on the hotel. You can often find inexpensive homes on Airbnb or similar sites if you’re traveling with a group and splitting the cost. There are also cheaper hotel chains that are perfectly nice as well. Think Best Western or Holiday Inn instead of Hilton or Marriott.  
This does require a bit of effort, so don’t be discouraged if it takes several weeks or months to find the right place. Many websites offer deal trackers or alerts to notify you when the price of a place goes down, which can save you some effort.

### **Understand What You Value and Be Willing to Say “No”**

Everything I’ve said here can apply to anyone, but really it applies to people who value travel. You might value something else, and that’s okay! Value is very subjective, which is why it’s important to know who you are and what matters to you.

It is also beneficial to say no sometimes. I have experienced situations where a friend wanted to go somewhere, but I didn’t find much value in it even if I found it exciting. Or sometimes people ask me to do things and I say no because I can tell it will cost too much.

I actually just said no to a trip to South Africa because while I’d love to go there someday, it’s just not in my budget right now. So I found an awesome (and very cheap!) trip to San Diego instead, which is another place on my growing travel list. And while they might seem very different, the two locations actually share some important things in common! As an Abundo advisor recently suggested to me: if you can’t afford a specific destination, you can probably find a cheaper one that is similar in many ways! They have beaches in San Diego and beaches in South Africa, and the sand feels the same under your feet.

To me, that is perfectly fine. When I say no to one thing and stand up for myself and my budget, I view it as saying yes to other things I would rather do more.

### **Overcome the Scarcity Mentality**

As a person who grew up low-income, I understand a mentality of not wanting to spend money and feeling like you don’t have a way to get it back. But I have also grown to realize at times that the scarcity mentality I have is based on a fear of the unknown.

When you plan well and follow common [travel hacking](https://abundowealth.com/blog/categories/travel-hacking) techniques like the ones I have shared or like the ones Abundo supplies in our client portal, you can learn how to prepare so that you reduce the number of unknowns. Reducing unknowns = reducing fear = confidence to achieve your goals.

I hope you will see more of the world soon. We only get to live this life once, so we might as well see what it has to offer.
