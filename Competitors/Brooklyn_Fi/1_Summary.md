# Brooklyn FI — Summary

> Brooklyn NY fee-only RIA branded as **BKFi**, co-founded by **Ally Jane (AJ) Ayers** (CFP®, EA, CEP) and **Shane Mason** (CPA/PFS, CFP®, CPWA®). **$563.1M AUM, 10 advisors, 10 employees across 10 branches** (distributed model), 0 DRPs. Three productized service tiers — **Start Up** (<$1M), **Core** ($1M-$10M), **Private** ($10M+) — bundling financial planning, investment management, and tax filing under one roof. Niche: **tech professionals and creatives**, with deep equity-comp specialty. Tagline: "Your wealth is complex. Working with us isn't."

## The headline

Brooklyn FI is the most **productized, team-built fee-only RIA** in the catalog that targets the tech-equity-comp niche. Where most niche-tech firms in our category run solo or duo, BKFi runs a ~21-person bench across financial planning, tax, and operations — with **147 years of combined experience** as the named credential. The three-tier model (Start Up / Core / Private) reads almost SaaS-like: clear price-by-tier, clear feature differences, clear AUM thresholds. Tax filing is bundled inside the firm, not outsourced — Dan Beyer (CPA) runs a 5-person tax team. This is what "financial planning + investments + tax under one roof" actually looks like when staffed properly.

Threat to USWM is **low**. BKFi's niche (creatives + tech employees with equity comp) overlaps minimally with USWM's two segments. But they're worth studying for two reasons: **(1)** the tier-based productization model is a teachable pattern, and **(2)** they prove a small founder-led firm registered ~2018 can scale to $563M AUM and a 21-person team within ~7 years.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Three productized tiers with named criteria.** Start Up / Core / Private isn't naming — it's gated by AUM thresholds and by what bundles in. Cleanest fee productization in the catalog.
- **In-house tax bench (5 CPAs).** Most fee-only RIAs outsource tax or treat it as referral. BKFi runs a real tax team headed by a CPA Director. Year-round proactive tax planning + 6-month tax forecasting at Core+ is meaningfully differentiated.
- **ECA credential push.** Almost every financial planner holds the Equity Compensation Associate credential alongside CFP®. Signals real niche commitment, not casual specialty marketing.
- **Two co-founders with complementary credentials.** AJ (CFP®/EA/CEP) covers planning + tax representation + estate; Shane (CPA/PFS/CFP®/CPWA®) covers tax + planning + UHNW. No single-credential blind spot.
- **"BKFi" shorthand.** Easier to type, remember, say. Brand discipline matters at scale.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **Narrow-niche dependency.** Tech equity comp is correlated to tech IPO/M&A windows. When tech cools, pipeline thins.
- **No 401(k)-specific funnel.** Tech employees often have a 401(k) plus equity; BKFi addresses equity, not the 401(k) optimization angle.
- **No early-adult / first-investor surface.** Start Up is for *startup employees* (pre-liquidity early-career tech with equity), not new-to-investing 22-year-olds.
- **"10 branches" is misleading.** It's a distributed model — 10 advisors each as their own paper branch.
- **Geographic brand concentration.** "Brooklyn" signals NYC even though delivery is virtual-national; may filter out West Coast tech prospects.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Three-tier productization is a teachable pattern.** USWM doesn't need three tiers on day one, but the *commitment* to specific price points + bundled services + AUM thresholds is the productization model. Publishing two tiers at launch would beat 90% of small-RIA peers.
2. **In-house tax integration is a real differentiator.** Beata's EA + NATP credentials let USWM sit alongside BKFi on tax sophistication. The bundle USWM should publish at Tier 2+: "Investment management + financial planning + tax preparation + year-round tax planning, all under one roof." This is what Beata is *for* in USWM's structure.
3. **Credentialed niche commitment.** BKFi's ECA-on-every-planner pattern shows that picking a niche means staffing it credentially. If USWM lands on a sub-niche over time, the credential push should follow.
4. **Founder credential complementarity.** Kean (MS Finance, CFA) + Beata (EA, NATP) is the kind of complementary stack BKFi runs. Lean into the pairing publicly — "Two professionals. One firm. Complementary credentials."

The narrowest single takeaway: **publish a tiered fee schedule.** Even rough tiers with stated AUM thresholds beat the gated-fee status most launch-stage RIAs default to.
