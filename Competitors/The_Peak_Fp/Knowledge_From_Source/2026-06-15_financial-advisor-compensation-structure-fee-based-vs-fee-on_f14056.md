## How To Begin Your Search for a Financial Advisor

The most important questions to ask a fee based or fee only financial advisor are:

1.  How does this particular financial advisor get paid?
2.  What type of business entity does this advisor work for?

How financial advisors get paid influences their incentives and ultimately dictates the **quality** of their advice.

Because the financial advising industry is so opaque, not all advisors are forthcoming about how they are paid.

You may wish to seek out a ***fee only financial advisor*** (who ***does not*** receive commissions in any form) but mistakenly work with a fee based or commission based financial advisor (who **will receive fees AND commissions**).

## **Commission Based vs Fee Based Financial Advisors**

For most of history financial advisors have been paid in a commission based manner.

This means that rather than being a “financial advisor” - someone who gives “financial advice" - they are actually a salesperson.

They have little financial expertise outside the domain of the exact product they sell.

Most of us are familiar with this - we meet a commission based financial advisor who presents as though they are economically and technically educated on all elements of investment markets, when in actuality all they know are life insurance products, annuities, or a small range of mutual funds.

## **Why should you avoid commission based financial planners?**

The commission based advisor makes money on the front end of any transaction they have with a client - and as such are incentivized to:

1) With hold information OR limit your exposure to other investment/financial options so that they can steer you into the financial product they are compensated for

and

2) To have a short-term transactional relationship and then move on to the next customer.

Intuitively consumers know that this is a bad model that ultimately rewards the commission based advisor at the expense of the consumer…

Towards the end of the 20th century, as the consumer expressed their distaste at being “sold”, the stale old commission based financial advisor began to morph into what are known as “fee based” financial advisors.

Fee based compensation is essentialy a hybrid financial advisor compensation structure.

The advisor still receives a commission for selling products, but can also at times charge an “hourly” or “project based” fee.

This essentially allows the fee based advisor to “switch hats” and honestly tell a client that they are not making commissions off of them when they charge the “hourly fee”. Even so, there are many problems with this model which we will dive deeper into below.

## **Fee Based Vs. Fee Only Financial Advisors**

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c5188ecc5485a64fe7_6646dd9fda5d33f2299ed66b_FA%252Bcompensation%252Bmethods.jpeg)

When financial advisors are paid commissions, they are not working for you, the client.

They are working for a company (brokerage, bank) that incentivizes the advisor to sell a **PRODUCT**.

As a consumer, you want to know if your advisor is working for **YOU** or working for **SOMEONE ELSE** when they present themselves to you.

To identify who a financial advisor is **REALLY** working for, I recommend focusing on these three things:

1.  **Is the advisor a fee-only advisor or fee-based advisor?**
2.  **Do they work under a Fiduciary or Suitability standard?**
3.  **How do you identify/verify how the advisor is compensated?**

## **How does a fee based financial advisor get paid?**

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c5188ecc5485a65006_6646de10c5635708aa3eac3c_Commission%252BBased%252BFinancial%252BAdvisor.png)

Fee based financial advisors are compensated via a **combination** of the fees you (the client) pay, as well as **brokerage commissions** for selling financial institution products such as insurance or mutual funds.

### What services are offered by Fee Based Financial Advisors?

Fee based advisors may offer services like **investment management**, **financial planning**, or **ongoing advice** (similar to fee only advisors), but will also position and **sell life insurance products**, **annuities**, **firm proprietary products**, and **mutual funds**.

While the fee based advisor will be incentivized to position their own firms proprietary products to you the client, it is important to note that this is not a bad option in all cases.

### Fee Based Advisors are ACTUALLY Commission Based Financial Advisors

Because fee based advisors are able to broker life insurance or annuity transactions, should one of those products be an optimal planning solution for you, the fee based financial advisor can complete the transaction in house rather than referring out.

This simplifies the process and streamlines financial planning matters since one expert can handle everything in house.

The downside of fee based compensation, as you will see when we discuss suitability vs fiduciary standards, is that you - the client - may not be presented with ALL possible solutions because the fee based advisor has a vested interest in showing you only the recommendations that make them the most money...

**NOTE TO READER:** Just as with the fee only model, the fee based model can also apply to financial planners in addition to financial advisors. Because of this, fee based financial advisor cost will be in line with that of a fee based financial advisor. It is important to note here that because certified financial planners are held to a fiduciary standard, there is no such thing as a fee based **certified financial planner**.

## **How does a fee only financial advisor get paid?**

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c5188ecc5485a6500d_6646cca6d5c37a50bab61090_Fee%252BOnly%252BFiduciary%252BFinancial%252BPlanner.png)

Fee only financial advisors are only compensated by **you, the client**, and **NEVER** receive any form of commission.

How the [fee only financial advisor is paid](https://www.thepeakfp.com/blog/flat-fee-financial-advisor) can differ by advisor - they may charge a **flat fee**, **hourly fees**, or a **percentage of assets under management**.

### What services are offered by Fee Only financial Advisors?

Fee only advisors may also offer a range of financial planning services that include [financial planning](https://www.thepeakfp.com/blog/sample-financial-plan), [investment management](https://www.thepeakfp.com/wealth-management-los-angeles), 401k or 401a [advice](https://www.thepeakfp.com/blog/401a-vs-401k), ongoing financial advice, or fee for service financial advice.

### What types of professionals may be considered Fee Only Financial Advisors?

Within the broader umbrella of fee only financial advisors you will also find:

1.  Advice only [financial planners](https://www.thepeakfp.com/blog/financial-advisor-vs-financial-planner). Advice only planners do not offer investment management but may offer fee only financial advice or flat fee financial planning on a dollars per hour basis.
2.  Fee only financial planners. Fee only financial planners are another term for fee only financial advisors who focus on retirement planning or financial planning.
3.  Fee only investment manager. Also known as fee only wealth advisor, this individual will focus primarily on money management and will most likely not offer financial planning or retirement planning services.
4.  Advice only financial advisors. Similar to advice only financial planners, the advice only financial advisor will not directly manage investment accounts even though they may give you advice as it relates to those accounts.

The key thing to note here is that because the advisor only gets paid by **YOU**, they therefore work **exclusively** for you.

In addition, it is a violation of the fee only advisor’s fiduciary duty to hold a life insurance license or receive any trailing commissions - even from prior positions in which they may have been working as fee based financial advisers...

Because the fee only advisor does not receive commissions in any form, they will not broker life insurance or annuity transactions.

Most fee only financial advisors will refer out for financial solutions such as annuities or life insurance products.

**NOTE TO READER:** The fee only model is not limited to financial advisors. There are fee only financial planners as well. Fee only financial planner cost and fee only financial planner rates will in most cases be the same as those charged by fee only financial advisors. Both fee only planners and fee only advisors benefit from reducing their possible conflicts of interest by engaging in the fee only model.

## **Fiduciary vs Suitability Standards**

### What Is The Suitability Standard?

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c4188ecc5485a64fdf_6646de39e3a73e89cb0101cd_Suitability%252BStandard.png)

#### **Fee based** advisors are held to a **“suitability” standard**.

Suitability requires that investment products such as an [annuity](https://www.thepeakfp.com/blog/what-is-the-primary-reason-for-buying-an-annuity) or a mutual fund sold for a commission must be **suitable** for the client **but need not be the best possible investment** product to meet the client's needs.

As mentioned above, in many cases the fee based advisor will position their firms proprietary products as the optimal solution for your financial needs when in actuality their may be better or lower fee solutions available in the marketplace.

### What Is The Fiduciary Standard?

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c5188ecc5485a64fec_6646de4d9e5c620ce8d6f239_fiduciary%252Bduty.jpeg)

#### **Fee only** advisors are held to a **“fiduciary” standard**.

The fiduciary standard requires that the advisor **put their clients' interests first and foremost**.

Part of “putting clients interests first and foremost” requires **eliminating bad incentives** such as commissions that could sway the advisor to recommend something less than the best possible solution for the clients' investment portfolio.

This will result in the advisor removing conflicts of interest, albeit with the tradeoff being that the advisor must refer out when situations arise that a life insurance or annuity product is indeed in your - the clients - best interest.

## **How to verify how do financial advisors get paid**

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6626dc6ca8fd4f898fc1181f/66b9c7c5188ecc5485a64ff6_6646de611bc8f6427f40f358_Verify%252BFinancial%252BAdvisor.png)

Most clients can spot a commission based financial advisor.

Your gut tells you to go with an advisor who is paid by you and not big banks or product placement firms.

But still, it's extremely difficult to tell between fee only and fee based advisors, especially when the advisor in questions isn't 100% transparent.

Here are three quick ways to verify how an advisor is compensated.

#### **Check their Form ADV**.

[Form ADV](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/form_adv.asp) is a compliance document hosted by [state](https://dfpi.ca.gov/check-out-your-investment-adviser/) or [federal](https://www.sec.gov/check-your-investment-professional) regulators that lists whether the advisor is fee based or fee only, the size of the practice, what other business activities they might participate in, whether they receive other compensation, and whether any disciplinary action has been levied against the company.

#### **Do some research**.

Most fee only advisors will be listed on fee only trade organization websites such as [FeeOnlyNetwork.com](https://www.feeonlynetwork.com/financial-advisor/eric-amzalag/), [NAPFA](https://www.napfa.org/firm/47520/36362), [XYPN](https://www.xyplanningnetwork.com/advisors/eric-amzalag-cfp/), or the [CFP](https://www.letsmakeaplan.org/find-a-cfp-professional/certified-professional-profile/3c4978fe-072d-4656-b399-2091645b8aad) website. If you cannot find the advisor on one of those listings, it's likely that the advisor is fee based rather than fee only.

#### **Ask the advisor directly**.

If the advisor isn't forthcoming or transparent, well that's all you need to know both about their quality of character, but also about how they are most likely compensated.

## **FAQ**

## How does a financial planner get paid?

Generally speaking, financial planners fall under the broader category of “Financial Advisor”. This means they are regulated by the same governing bodies and operate under the same fiduciary or suitability standard, depending on how they or their firm charges its’ fees.

The best form of financial planner is an independent, fee only, fiduciary, certified financial planner. As a CFP they will have above average education and work experience. As independent financial planners they will not be constrained by the terrible incentive model of the large corporate brokerage firms. And as a Fee Only Fiduciary they will remove conflicts of interest and therefore be able to work as a fiduciary (in your best interest). Generally speaking this means financial planners will be paid some combination of hourly, retainer, or project based fees as well as a percentage of assets that they manage on your behald.

## How do financial advisors get paid?

Financial advisors are paid via some combination of: commissions for the sale of financial products, a percentage of investment assets they manage, financial planning fees, or hourly/retainer based fees. The specific combination of fees that an individual financial advisor charges will be the determining factor whether they will be able to call themselves a fee only or fee based financial advisor…

## What does Fee only vs fee based mean?

Fee only vs fee based refers to whether a financial advisor is paid commissions or not. Fee only financial advisors can NEVER receive any form of sales related compensation such as commissions, kickbacks, referral fees, etc. This allows the fee only financial advisor to work as a fiduciary on behalf of their client, meaning a better, more fulfilling, safer relationship for the client.

Fee based financial advisors receive a combination of hourly fees in addition to commissions and can therefore obscure the conflicts of interest that might exist between them and their client. Commissions and the ugly incentives they create are why we recommend not to work with fee based financial advisors if you can avoid it…
