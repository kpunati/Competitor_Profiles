# Asset Map — Summary

> Major B2B SaaS for advisors — **Asset-Map, Inc.**, Philadelphia, PA — visual financial-planning software with **2.14M people mapped**, **$1.6T in financial instruments mapped**, named to the **2026 WealthTech100** by FinTech Global, SOC 2 Type II certified, with fully published transparent tier pricing ($1,699 / $2,269 / Custom annually). This is a **VENDOR adjacency, not a competitor**: Asset Map is named on the homepage of [Intrepid Wealth Partners](../Intrepid_Wealth_Partners/1_Summary.md) as part of their RIA tech stack. International (US + UK/Europe + Africa + India/Asia-Pacific).

## The headline

Asset Map is **not on our threat radar** — they're a tool we could buy. It's profiled for two structural reasons: (1) it's the *third* B2B SaaS in our catalog (alongside [Couplr AI](../Couplr_AI/1_Summary.md) and the [Kitces](../Kitces/1_Summary.md) education platform), making it useful for understanding what advisor-side procurement looks like; and (2) it is the *gold standard for fee-transparent B2B pricing* against which we should benchmark our own consumer-side pricing approach. The site publishes three tier prices with feature deltas, a 5% annual-billing discount, a 30-day money-back guarantee, and a customer-role-aware demo flow that asks "Broker-Dealer Advisor / RIA / Hybrid RIA / Bank/Credit Union Advisor / Back Office" before routing the prospect to the right sales motion. This is what category-leading SaaS marketing looks like in 2026.

## What they sell, to whom

A **visual financial-planning platform** that turns spreadsheet meetings into single-page interactive financial maps. Three product tiers:

| Tier | Annual Investment | Customer | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Professional** | **$1,699/year** | Solo / small-practice advisor | Know Your Client · Analysis + Advice · Present + Collaborate · Growth + Success |
| **Elite** *(MOST POPULAR)* | **$2,269/year** | Premium-brand single advisor | Everything in Professional + White-Labeled Platform · Customized Data Gathering · Enhanced Relationship Management · White-Glove Support + Training |
| **Firm** *(BEST VALUE)* | **Custom / Let's Talk** | 3+ advisor team / RIA / Bank | Everything above + Practice Management · Practice Insights + Analytics · Firm-Specific Branding · Data Migration · Volume Discounts |

5% off with annual (vs monthly) billing. 30-day risk-free trial with money-back guarantee. All plans are annual term commitments.

**Product framework**: a six-question planning model — **Who · Why · What/Where · If · When · How** — surfaced as named map features:
- **Relationship Maps** (Who) — financial connections + people
- **Priorities** (Why) — client values and intentions
- **The Asset-Map** (What/Where) — single-page life inventory
- **Signals** (If) — risk and gap detection
- **Target-Maps** (When) — timeline and funding gaps
- **Drafts** (How) — before-and-after scenario presentation

**Stated positioning**: *"the only visual-first planning platform built for how humans actually think, whether you're using it standalone, alongside eMoney or MoneyGuidePro, for discovery meetings, or client presentations."* — explicitly compatible with the two dominant incumbent planning tools (eMoney + MoneyGuidePro), positioning Asset-Map as additive rather than displacing.

**Customer types served** (from the demo-form taxonomy): Broker-Dealer Advisor · RIA · Hybrid RIA · Bank or Credit Union Advisor · Back Office / Operational.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **The cleanest published-tier B2B pricing in our catalog.** Specific dollar prices, MOST POPULAR and BEST VALUE badges, 5% annual discount, 30-day money-back guarantee, named feature deltas tier-to-tier. Pricing pages don't get more SaaS-correct than this.
- **The "compatible with eMoney + MoneyGuidePro" positioning.** Most challenger SaaS in financial-services tech tries to *displace* incumbents; Asset Map positions as *additive*, which dramatically reduces switching cost objections during the sale. Worth borrowing the framing.
- **A six-question framework that maps to product features.** Who · Why · What/Where · If · When · How. Each question is a feature module. This is the cleanest "framework as product taxonomy" execution in our catalog — beats Foundry Financial's "Foundry Framework™" and KDA Inc.'s "KDA Promise" both in specificity and in how-it-shapes-the-product.
- **8 named-with-credentials testimonials with firm names.** Martin V. Higgins CFP®, ChFC®, CLU®, AEP®, LUTCF®, RHU® (President, Family Wealth Management) is the strongest credentialing format in our catalog. Compare to anonymized "Financial Advisor" testimonials at Couplr AI, or first-name-+-last-initial at My CPA Coach. Asset Map shows the gold standard.
- **A multi-step demo form** that pre-segments the prospect by role *before* collecting contact info. Reduces sales-cycle friction and lets the team route to the right SE.
- **Real-scale claims with specific numbers.** 2.14M people mapped · $1.6T in financial instruments · 30% revenue increase for new advisors. The third number is a marketing claim that needs a footnote; the first two are operational metrics that signal scale.
- **Industry recognition prominently featured.** 2026 WealthTech100 by FinTech Global, announced April 8, 2026, on the company blog. Specific award + specific issuing body + specific date is the right way to display this.
- **A separate `asset-map.store`** for branded merchandise — small touch that signals the platform has built a community of advocates rather than just a customer list.
- **A `/resources/referral-program`** — converts existing customers into the sales force at scale.
- **International landing pages** (UK/Europe, Africa, India/Asia-Pacific) — suggests real international revenue, not just a SaaS that "supports global users."
- **Compliance posture as feature.** SOC 2 Type II + FINRA + SEC compliance is asserted with the auditor relationships implied. For an advisor-data product, this is the price-of-entry trust signal and Asset Map publishes it prominently.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **No published team page on captured surfaces** (the `/about/team` page wasn't pulled). For a B2B SaaS at this scale, the team page would normally be public and full of headshots.
- **No published customer count** beyond "thousands of advisors." Compare to competitor MoneyGuidePro which routinely cites named-firm customers and total subscriber counts. The "2.14M people mapped" stat is the closest customer-scale proxy.
- **The "30% revenue increase for new advisors"** claim needs a footnote. The other two big-stat callouts (2.14M / $1.6T) are operational; this one is a marketing claim about customer outcomes and would benefit from a citation or methodology note.
- **The advisor-side B2B SaaS category is genuinely competitive.** eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, Right Capital, Holistiplan, Snappy Kraken, Wealthbox CRM, Nitrogen (formerly Riskalyze), Asset-Map, and now Couplr AI all serve the same advisor procurement layer. Asset Map's positioning is "visual-first / pre-discovery / additive-to-incumbents" which is a defensible niche but not an obvious moat.
- **Pricing tiers serve solo advisors and firms, but the gap between Elite ($2,269) and Firm (custom) is wide** — a 2-3 advisor practice doesn't have a published price, which means they need a sales call. Tax Alchemy and My CPA Coach have the same gap issue.

## What this means for our entry

Asset Map is **not a competitor we will encounter prospect-side**. Its strategic value to us is:

1. **As a candidate planning tool** — if we adopt Asset Map (alongside eMoney or MoneyGuidePro or solo), we can name it on our homepage the way Intrepid Wealth Partners does. The "named tech stack" positioning we identified in the Intrepid profile gets meaningfully stronger if our planning tool is a recognizable WealthTech100 brand.
2. **As a B2B-pricing benchmark.** Three published tiers with specific dollar amounts + a "Most Popular" anchor + a 30-day guarantee + 5% annual discount is the SaaS-correct pricing-page template. We can adapt directly for our own consumer-side pricing.
3. **As a "compatibility framing" reference.** The way Asset Map positions ("works alongside eMoney or MoneyGuidePro") is the right way for a challenger to compete with incumbents. For us against the major roboadvisors and incumbent wealth managers, the analogue is: "works alongside your existing 401(k) / brokerage / robo." Don't pitch displacement; pitch addition.
4. **As a six-question framework reference.** Who · Why · What/Where · If · When · How is the cleanest planning-framework taxonomy in our catalog. For our retail product, an analogous framework worth building: "Today / Soon / Someday / What-if" or similar — make the planning model legible.

What we should explicitly *not* take: the "30% revenue increase" claim style without methodology footnotes (the SEC Marketing Rule restricts unsupported outcome claims for RIAs), and the "we plug into your existing planning tool" framing applied verbatim (we're the financial-life primary, not a tool that augments another).

The narrowest single takeaway: **Asset Map's pricing page is the closest B2B-SaaS-correct pricing template in our catalog**, and the **6-question planning framework + named product modules** is the cleanest framework-as-product execution. Both worth saving as reference artifacts.

A second narrow note for the catalog: **the pipeline's enrich-adv.ts pickBestMatch returned a false-positive on this firm**, matching "Asset Map" against "Sugar Maple Asset Management, LLC" via prefix-token fuzz. This is the *second* false-positive in our pipeline (Streamline Planning was the first, matched against an unrelated NY DBA umbrella). The fix is to tighten pickBestMatch to require exact whole-word token equality before declaring a confident match. Documented in this profile's metadata.
