# Peoples Capital Group — Summary

> New Jersey passive-real-estate-syndication firm founded **2013** by **Seth Martinez + Aaron Fragnito** — **109+ investors, $114M+ transaction volume, 13+ years in business, 300+ real-estate transactions, $25M+ AUM** — operating Reg D-style syndications on Northern NJ / NYC-Metro Class-B apartment complexes (Paterson, Rahway). Aaron Fragnito hosts the **Passive Cash Flow Podcast** + **NJ Real Estate Network (NJREN)** as personality-led top-of-funnel content. NOT a registered investment adviser (no IAPD match correctly returned by pipeline) — a vertically integrated real-estate investment company that operates outside the RIA regulatory perimeter via private placements.

## The headline

PCG sits in the **"wealth-building through real estate instead of markets"** adjacency to our retail wealth-management business. The pitch is explicit: "become less reliant on the stock market." The product is named, illustrated deal-by-deal on the homepage (11-unit Paterson NJ at 24% annualized cash-on-cash · 27-unit Rahway NJ at 11%+ targeted return, 15%+ targeted IRR · 25-unit Paterson at 10%+ targeted return), and gated behind a Discovery Call → Approval to Invest funnel that pre-qualifies accredited investors.

The target prospect for PCG is **a 40-50 high-income professional or business owner with $50K+ to allocate**, who's choosing real-estate syndications over (or alongside) public-market wealth management. That prospect overlaps with our 40-50 401(k) segment — but the *product* doesn't compete with ours; it competes with our prospect's choice to allocate to real estate versus stay in the market. **Different decision tree.** Threat is **low** — but PCG belongs to a recurring archetype we keep seeing in this catalog (PCG, [Samo_Financial](../Samo_Financial/1_Summary.md), [Dealmakers](../Dealmakers/1_Summary.md), [Karlton_Dennis](../Karlton_Dennis/1_Summary.md)) of *content-led personality-driven alternative wealth-building brands* that compete for the same prospect's attention as we do without competing for their advisory dollars.

## Who they are

- **Founded**: **2013** by Seth Martinez and Aaron Fragnito
- **Size**: Two named principals + an unspecified team. No total headcount published.
- **Where they're based**: Chatham, NJ (Seth's residence per About page); Flemington, NJ (Aaron's residence). Single operational footprint; deal flow concentrated in Northern NJ / NYC-Metro.
- **How they're funded**: Founder-led real-estate investment LLC. 8-figure portfolio of real estate held with clients. No external capital visible.
- **Regulatory**: NOT an SEC/state-registered investment adviser. Operates Reg D-style real-estate syndications (likely Rule 506(c) given the public marketing) for accredited investors only. Site disclaimer is explicit: "This is not a solicitation for Funds. This feature and all content on our website for information purposes and not financial advice." At $25M AUM PCG is well below the federal $100M SEC threshold; depending on activities they may operate as an Exempt Reporting Adviser (ERA) under §203(m) of the Investment Advisers Act, but more likely they're a pure RE syndicator that sits outside the Adviser-Act perimeter entirely.
- **Trust signals**: 109+ investors, $114M+ transaction volume, 300+ real-estate transactions, 13+ years operating history. Three named deals with specific Capital Improvement / Reposition Value / IRR numbers — concrete enough to be verifiable.

## What they sell, to whom

**The product**: passive equity interest in NJ apartment-building syndications. Each offering: a discrete Class-B multifamily property in Northern NJ that PCG sources at a discount, rehabs ($65K–$335K capital improvements per deal in the captured set), repositions, and operates — distributing cash flow to investor LPs.

**Stated deal-level economics** (from homepage prior offerings):

| Deal | Capital Raised | # Investors | Capital Improvements | Targeted Return | Actual / Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 11-unit Paterson NJ | $400,000 | 10 | $250,000 | 10%+ | **24% annualized cash-on-cash achieved** |
| 27-unit Rahway NJ | $1,325,000 | 19 | $335,000 | 11%+ return / 15%+ IRR | "Ahead of schedule" |
| 25-unit Paterson NJ | $1,035,000 | 18-19 | $65,000 | 10%+ return / 15%+ IRR | "Ahead of schedule" |

**Buyer onboarding** (4-step funnel published on homepage):
1. **Discovery Call** — qualification on investment needs and accredited-investor credentials
2. **Approval to Invest** — PCG processes the information and matches to a specific offering
3. **Investment** — closes, with monthly updates + quarterly cash-flow reporting
4. **Wealth Growth & Preservation** — passive hold

**Target customer**: Accredited investors (likely $200K+ income / $1M+ NW per Reg D) looking for passive real-estate exposure with $50K+ to allocate per deal. Implicit minimum is $50K-$100K based on capital-raised-divided-by-investor-count math.

**Bonus mechanic**: 10% of PCG's asset-management fee donated to World Vision, St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, and Wounded Warrior Project — a charity component that doubles as a values-aligned trust signal.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Four-axis rubric: **Reach 2** (real podcast — Passive Cash Flow + NJREN — with real audience in the NJ real-estate community; mainstream-press footprint not captured; geography largely NJ), **Segment overlap 2** (the 40-50 high-income-professional prospect who's evaluating real-estate syndications is materially the same prospect we want for our 401(k) and mid-career segment — but they're choosing a *different product*), **Momentum 2** (13 years operating history, $114M cumulative transaction volume, active podcast cadence, multiple "ahead of schedule" deal callouts), **Capital backing 1** (founder-led, no external capital) → sum **7 → medium** — but classified **low** for our specific catalog because the *product overlap* is zero.

The honest framing: PCG isn't going to take a prospect *from us* — but a prospect who hires PCG isn't hiring us, because they're choosing the real-estate-syndication thesis over the markets-allocation thesis.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Deal-level economics published with specific numbers.** "$400K capital raised, $250K capital improvements, 24% annualized cash-on-cash achieved" is the kind of concrete attribution that builds trust in a category that's often opaque about returns. Most real-estate syndicators publish glossy "lifestyle" copy; PCG publishes specific Paterson-NJ-11-unit deal numbers.
- **A defined 4-step funnel** prominently on the homepage. Discovery Call → Approval to Invest → Investment → Wealth Growth & Preservation. The clarity of the process is itself a trust mechanic — the prospect knows what they're signing up for at each step.
- **Two named principals with substantive bios.** Seth Martinez (medical-billing co-founder background, CPC Medical Billing 2003, 7-figure exit 2009) and Aaron Fragnito (300+ RE transactions, host of Passive Cash Flow Podcast + NJREN, former adjunct professor at Rowan University teaching "Real Estate Entrepreneurship"). Real, verifiable founder track-records.
- **An ROI calculator** on the homepage as a lead-magnet — $50K to $10M initial investment, 3-20 years, calculated outcome. Concrete and interactive.
- **A charity component** as a trust-and-values signal. Specific causes named (World Vision, St. Jude, Wounded Warrior) with a specific percentage (10% of asset-management fee) — not vague "we give back."
- **Aaron's podcast + the NJREN community** as the audience-building lever. Same playbook as Karlton Dennis (YouTube), Jonathan Jay (Business Buying Strategies Podcast), Sven Carlin (Modern Value Investing podcast) — content-as-acquisition is universal.
- **A self-disclaimer at the right depth.** "This is not a solicitation for Funds. This feature and all content on our website for information purposes and not financial advice." That's the standard Reg D-marketing-rule scaffolding done correctly.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **Geographic concentration is extreme.** Every captured deal is in Northern NJ (Paterson + Rahway). Single-market exposure to NJ apartment-building economics. A regional rent-control change or a Newark-area real-estate downturn would simultaneously hit every deal in the book.
- **No published deal-level loss data.** Three "ahead of schedule" deals are shown — none "behind schedule," none "loss-of-principal." Either the firm has never had a bad deal in 13 years (unlikely given the asset class), or the under-performing deals aren't featured on the homepage (most likely).
- **No fee schedule published.** Acquisition fee, asset management fee, disposition fee, promote / waterfall structure — all opaque. The mention of "10% of PCG's Asset Management Fee" donated to charity implies a fee exists, but the actual percentage isn't shown.
- **Key-person risk on the named principals.** Seth and Aaron *are* the firm. If either steps back, deal sourcing and investor relations collapse.
- **No 401(k) / IRA-rollover-into-real-estate funnel visible.** A common play in this category is the Self-Directed-IRA partnership that lets retirement assets invest in syndications. PCG doesn't surface this if they do it.
- **Regulatory ambiguity.** Operating Reg D-style syndications at scale ($25M AUM, $114M+ cumulative volume, 109+ investors) is squarely within standard private-placement market practice — but the firm doesn't disclose its Form D filings, doesn't name its broker-dealer (if any), doesn't show the offering documents publicly. All standard for the category, but it's an information-asymmetry the prospect has to swallow.

## What this means for our entry

PCG is **not on our threat radar for advisory clients**, but the structural lessons:

1. **Specific deal-level numbers beat lifestyle copy every time.** The Paterson-NJ-11-unit "24% annualized cash-on-cash achieved" callout is more compelling than any "wealth preservation" headline. For our RIA, the analog is: *publish actual client-portfolio-style examples* (anonymized, SEC-compliant) — not just "our investment philosophy." Specificity converts.
2. **The 4-step funnel as a homepage trust artifact** is a useful pattern. Many advisor sites in our catalog have *no* explicit "what happens after you contact us" diagram. PCG shows the buyer the whole arc.
3. **A real ROI calculator on the homepage.** We should ship one. For our wealth-management product, the calculator might be "what does a $X/month allocation to a diversified portfolio compound to by year Y?" — straightforward, interactive, lead-capturing.
4. **Charity component with named causes + named percentages** is a values-trust mechanic. Worth considering, structurally — though for an RIA the math is different (RIAs typically donate by % of profits rather than % of fee revenue).
5. **The "real estate as wealth strategy" thesis is alive enough** in 2026 to support 13-year-old firms with $114M in cumulative deal volume. If our prospects are routinely encountering PCG / Samo / BiggerPockets content, we should have a coherent answer to "why are you choosing diversified-allocation over my-buddy-told-me-about-Paterson-apartment-buildings." Worth a paragraph in our own messaging.

What we should explicitly *not* take: the no-fee-schedule posture, the no-bad-deal-stories pattern, the single-geography concentration (we are not constrained to NJ apartment buildings; we can deliver national reach from day one), and the Reg D regulatory ambiguity (we will be a registered RIA with a Form ADV; our regulatory posture is the opposite of PCG's).

The narrowest single takeaway: **publish specific examples with concrete numbers.** PCG's Paterson-NJ-11-unit deal page is more useful as a trust artifact than 90% of the wealth-firm About pages in our catalog.
