# Money Guy / Abound Wealth Management — Summary

> Major personality-led personal-finance brand (**Brian Preston CPA/CFP®/PFS + Bo Hanson CFA/CFP®**) running The Money Guy Show + 4 spin-off shows across podcast and YouTube, with a multi-tier product stack converging on the **Financial Order of Operations® (FOO)** signature framework. Underlying RIA: **Abound Wealth Management, LLC** — Franklin, TN — **$1.95B AUM, 26 advisors, 41 employees, 3 branches, 0 DRPs.** Two-domain architecture similar to [PWL_Capital](../Pwl_Capital/1_Summary.md): moneyguy.com is the content surface; Abound separately operates the regulated wealth-management business that the "Become a Client" CTA routes into.

## The headline

The Money Guy Show is one of the **biggest personality-led personal-finance brands in our catalog with a real regulated RIA underneath it** — same structural archetype as PWL Capital (RIA + Rational Reminder podcast + Ben Felix YouTube), Ritholtz Wealth (RIA + The Compound + multiple host podcasts), but with **a meaningfully different product strategy**: where PWL and Ritholtz sell wealth management *as the conversion product*, Money Guy explicitly sells a **multi-tier course ladder** ($49 FOO course → Know Your Number course → Millionaire Mission book → free calculators/tools) *alongside* the AUM business. The result is a much wider funnel — Money Guy can monetize content-only customers at the $49 course tier without requiring them to graduate to the $1.95B AUM business.

The threat to a retail RIA serving both early-adult and 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segments is **medium-high**. Four-axis rubric: **Reach 3** (one of the biggest weekly personal-finance podcasts + active YouTube + multi-show franchise + 5 named shows with episode cadence), **Segment overlap 3** (Financial Order of Operations explicitly addresses both segments — early-adult "where do I put my next dollar" and mid-career "401(k) optimization, debt strategy, retirement readiness" are core content), **Momentum 3** (active weekly cadence + recent book launch + recent show launches like "Making a Millionaire" + the underlying RIA at $1.95B AUM is at "scale-up" not "boutique" size), **Capital backing 1** (founder-led, no PE backer visible) → sum **10 → high** in raw rubric terms. Downgraded to **medium-high** because their conversion-to-RIA-client funnel is gated through Franklin TN-based wealth management and our prospects can pick from us without leaving the audience-attention space.

## How big a threat

**Medium-high.** Four-axis rubric: **Reach 3 + Segment overlap 3 + Momentum 3 + Capital backing 1 = 10 raw** (high band). Downgraded one band to medium-high because:
- The conversion engine from "watch the show" to "become an Abound client" requires geographic and asset-tier qualifications that won't apply to most content-audience prospects
- The audience overlap is real but the *advisory-client* competition is smaller than the rubric suggests; Money Guy's content audience is much larger than its 26-advisor practice can serve, so they're primarily competing for *attention*, not *clients*

For our retail RIA, this means: a prospect researching wealth management who finds Money Guy *will* be influenced by the FOO framework before they encounter our pitch. Our messaging needs to acknowledge this — not by competing with it, but by extending it ("if you've already worked through the Financial Order of Operations, here's what comes next").

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **The Financial Order of Operations® (FOO) framework** is the single most useful piece of category-shaping IP in the catalog. A named 9-step framework with a registered trademark, sold as a $49 course, that doubles as the brand's editorial spine — every show, blog post, and free tool references and reinforces it. Compare to Foundry's "Foundry Framework™" (asserted but thin), KDA's "KDA Promise" (a 3-sentence values statement), or Asset Map's six-question planning model (good but not branded as the conversion product). FOO is the gold standard.
- **A real product ladder** spanning free → $49 → $X → Become-a-Client. Each rung has a different conversion target. Free tools convert email subscribers. The $49 FOO course converts the cost-conscious DIY audience. Know Your Number + Millionaire Mission convert the "I want to go deeper" audience. The AUM business converts the "I want it done for me" audience. Few firms in our catalog operate this many rungs cleanly.
- **Dual-credential founding pair.** Brian (CPA/CFP®/PFS) + Bo (CFA/CFP®) covers tax + planning + investments + behavioral with named credentials. The CPA/PFS credential on Brian is rare — fewer than 1% of CPAs hold the PFS and it's specifically the AICPA's planning credential. Worth borrowing as a credential-stack signal if any of our founding team holds it.
- **Five-show production strategy.** Most content-led RIAs operate one show; Money Guy operates five distinct formats serving different audiences. "Financial Advisors React" + "Financial Advisors Explain" extend the brand into adjacent advisor-side content that builds B2B credibility. "Making a Millionaire" is the long-arc narrative format that builds emotional connection. "Ask Money Guy" is the listener-response format that lowers content-production cost. Smart portfolio.
- **Comprehensive free-tool stack.** Wealth Multiplier · Compound Interest · 20/3/8 Car Rule · House Affordability · Net Worth. Each tool is a separate URL with separate SEO surface. The 20/3/8 Car Rule is specifically a Money-Guy-branded heuristic that other personal-finance content references. Owning a *named rule* is even more durable than owning a framework.
- **"Become a Client" is a single CTA** that the entire content brand funnels into. No competing offers, no confusion. Same conversion-discipline as Foundry Financial's "Are We a Good Fit?" pattern but at much larger scale.
- **Topic taxonomy maps directly to product.** The site's editorial taxonomy (Financial Planning / Investing / Budgeting / Debt Management / Money Mindset / Tax Planning / Retirement / Estate / Wealth Management) is exactly the structure of the FOO course steps. Content navigation = product navigation.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **No fee transparency on the AUM side.** "Become a Client" routes to Abound Wealth Management but we couldn't capture the actual fee schedule (Cloudflare 504 on the about page). Probably published on the Abound site itself but they keep the moneyguy.com content surface clean of pricing — possibly intentional to avoid associating the content brand with a specific dollar figure.
- **No early-adult-specific first-step.** FOO step 1 starts at "high-interest debt" — which assumes the prospect already has debt to pay off. A 22-year-old setting up their first investment account doesn't naturally find step-1 useful. The first-investment-account moment is the gap.
- **Cloudflare bot protection on the about page** (returned 504 Gateway Timeout to Playwright). Same pattern we've seen on ~10 firms now. Not a content vulnerability — a competitive-intel friction point.
- **Key-person risk is real even with two founders.** The brand is Brian and Bo together. If either steps back, the show identity changes substantially. Their visible succession plan is the 26-advisor team at Abound, not a content-team backstop.

## What this means for our entry

Money Guy is **medium-high threat**. Strategic takeaways:

1. **A named, branded framework with a registered trademark + a paid course at the bottom of the funnel** is the single most valuable IP move in this entire catalog. Whether we ship one at launch or build to it, the FOO model is what to aim at. The pattern: take a 5-9 step process you teach clients anyway, name it, trademark it, sell it as a $49 standalone course, and use it as the editorial spine for everything else.
2. **Five-show portfolio is overkill at launch but the pattern is right.** Start with one weekly show, plan the spin-offs (Q&A format, reaction format, long-arc narrative format) as a 12-24 month roadmap. Each spin-off serves a different audience archetype.
3. **Named heuristics (20/3/8 Car Rule) are even more durable than frameworks.** A single memorable rule with three numbers in it (20% down / 3-year loan / 8% of gross income) gets cited by other personal-finance creators — extending the brand's reach without paid acquisition. Worth attempting if we have an unusual heuristic to propose.
4. **The CPA/PFS + CFA/CFP dual credential pair** is a credentialing model worth borrowing. Our founding team should aim for complementary credentials (planning + investments + tax + behavioral) rather than redundant ones (three CFPs).
5. **Single conversion CTA across the content brand.** "Become a Client" is the only commercial CTA. Everything else is free or course-tier. Worth deciding before launch what *our* single CTA is — and disciplining the site around it.

What we should explicitly *not* copy: the geographic concentration (Franklin TN only, 3 branches), the no-fee-transparency posture on the content surface (we should publish), and the assumed-existing-debt entry point in the FOO step 1.

The narrowest single takeaway: **a named, trademarked framework sold as a paid course is the most durable content-monetization mechanic we can build.** FOO is the model. Worth designing ours from day one.
