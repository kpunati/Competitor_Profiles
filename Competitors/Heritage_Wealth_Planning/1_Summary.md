# Heritage Wealth Planning — Summary

> Solo **Josh Scandlen, CFP® Practitioner** with a Master's in Personal Financial Planning, running a **flat-fee planning-only practice** with fully published fees tiered by net worth: **$1,250 / $2,500 / $5,000** + a **$250 "Can I Retire?" course**. Heavy anti-AUM-industry voice. Per IAPD, the firm's RIA registration is **INACTIVE (CRD 293260, $0 reported AUM, 0 branches, 0 DRPs)** — Josh has moved operations into the education-publisher carve-out, selling planning-as-flat-fee-engagements + courses + books rather than ongoing advisory.

## The headline

Heritage Wealth Planning is **the cleanest example in our catalog of a firm that *deliberately exited* the registered-RIA perimeter** to operate fully under the education-publisher carve-out (same regulatory shape as Karlton Dennis's Tax Alchemy, ClearValue Investing, Sven Carlin — but distinctive because Heritage previously held active registration and went INACTIVE rather than never having registered).

Josh is doing several things in tension with each other:
- Calling the product "Financial Planning" with named deliverables (Roth conversions, tax analysis, retirement scenarios, estate documents)
- Charging flat-fee tiers based on net worth complexity
- Listing himself as a CFP® Practitioner with a Master's in PFP
- ...while the underlying firm's RIA registration is INACTIVE in IAPD

This is regulatory grey territory. The CFP® designation is held by Josh personally and is regulated by the CFP Board — that part is fine. But the firm offering tiered planning engagements without active registration is the kind of arrangement that depends on the engagements being structured as one-time deliverables (which they appear to be) rather than ongoing advice. Worth noting but not over-interpreting.

Threat to our retail RIA is **low** — Josh targets a different prospect (do-it-yourself retirees who explicitly want to avoid AUM-based advisors) and his content has a niche conservative-political stance ("get my fellow right wingers to live in reality") that filters his audience aggressively. We'd never compete for the same prospect.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Fully published flat-fee tier schedule on its own page.** $1,250 / $2,500 / $5,000 with clear net-worth qualifiers. Single most fee-transparent planning-only firm in our catalog. Combined with the $250 course as the bottom rung, the price ladder is legible end-to-end.
- **Tiered by complexity (net worth), not by services.** All three tiers cover the same deliverables; the price gap reflects engagement complexity. Cleaner mechanic than tiering by services (which forces the buyer into a service-comparison decision rather than a complexity-self-assessment).
- **30-day money-back guarantee on the course.** Standard creator-economy move done correctly.
- **Single contact channel.** Email only (Josh@HeritageWealthPlanning.com). No phone, no calendar widget, no chat. Forces a written first contact which qualifies for prospect literacy + commitment.
- **Anti-AUM positioning sentence.** "From someone who doesn't get paid to sell you stuff" is a direct, falsifiable claim that segments the audience instantly. Most fee-only RIAs hedge this; Josh is direct.
- **Net-worth-tier pricing is segment-disciplined.** Compare to Peak FP (also tiers by AUM), Tax Alchemy (band $20K-$150K). Heritage's $1,250-$5,000 range hits a different prospect — middle-class-to-upper-middle-class retirees with $200K to $5M+, not the HNW Tax Alchemy market.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **The INACTIVE RIA registration is the headline risk.** The firm is operating in the education-publisher carve-out, which works for one-time deliverables but is fragile if the engagement looks like ongoing advice. State securities regulators occasionally test the boundary.
- **Political stance in content.** Josh's blog includes copy like "get my fellow right wingers to live in reality, not made up globalist fear mongering." That's a filter — it converts the aligned audience but eliminates a much larger neutral audience. For most firms this would be a vulnerability; Josh is treating it as a feature.
- **Solo operator with no successor.** If Josh steps back, the firm ends. No team page, no successor language.
- **No fee schedule for ongoing advice** — because there is no ongoing advice. Prospects looking for an AUM-managed relationship won't convert; Heritage is structurally not that product.
- **Religious framing in copy** ("Ecclesiastes 3:12") — same audience-filtering pattern as the political framing. Effective for the aligned segment, eliminating for everyone else.

## What this means for our entry

Heritage is **not on our threat radar** — different prospect, different product, different audience filter. Strategic takeaways:

1. **Tiered-by-complexity pricing** (not tiered-by-services) is a useful structural pattern. Three tiers all delivering the same outcome, priced by the underlying complexity. For our retail RIA: rather than "Basic Plan / Premium Plan / Elite Plan" with different services, consider "Standard Engagement / Complex Engagement / Multi-Entity Engagement" all delivering the same wealth-management product but priced for the back-end complexity.
2. **The $250 course at the bottom of the ladder + the $1,250 planning tier as the first paid engagement = a useful funnel pattern.** Course captures the cost-conscious DIY prospect; planning captures the prospect who wants something done for them. Both products under one brand. Worth modeling.
3. **An explicit single contact channel** (email only) is a friction-as-quality-signal move. Probably not right for our retail RIA at launch (we want lower barrier to entry), but worth knowing as a positioning lever for later.
4. **Anti-AUM positioning sentence.** "From someone who doesn't get paid to sell you stuff" works for Josh but only because he genuinely doesn't take AUM. We can't make the same claim — we will be AUM-based. The lesson is *commit to one regulatory/fee posture and message it directly*, not "borrow Josh's specific sentence."

What we should explicitly *not* take: the INACTIVE registration status (we'll be SEC-registered from day one), the political/religious filtering language (we want a neutral broad audience), the no-team / solo positioning, and the no-ongoing-advice structure (we are explicitly building an ongoing-advisory practice).

The narrowest single takeaway: **the firm's INACTIVE registration is the most useful single observation in this profile.** It tells us that the "ex-RIA-now-content-publisher" archetype is a real category — Heritage, Karlton Dennis (Tax Alchemy), Sven Carlin, ClearValue Investing all operate in this regulatory shape. It's a category we should be aware of as we choose our own regulatory posture.
