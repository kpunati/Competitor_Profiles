# Wiser Wealth Management — Summary

> Marietta GA fee-only fiduciary RIA — **$513.6M AUM, 11 employees, 5 advisors, 0 DRPs.** Note: brand domain wiserwealth.com timed out on capture; live site is **wiserinvestor.com**. Notable for an **unusually broad six-niche menu** on the homepage: Retirement · Business Owners · **Single Again Community** (divorce-recovery) · **Airline Pilots** · Legacy · Inheritance. "No product sales or commissions."

## The headline

Wiser is a $513M small/mid fee-only RIA that has chosen the opposite strategy from most niche specialists — instead of committing to one segment, they explicitly serve **six**. "Single Again Community" (a divorce-recovery sub-segment) and "Airline Pilots" are the two unusual ones; most catalogs don't surface either at the home-page level. The strategy is risky (niche dilution) but the AUM-per-advisor (~$100M each) suggests it's working economically.

Threat to USWM **as a competitor**: low — different region, no segment overlap. Strategic value: Wiser is the catalog's clearest example of a small-firm **multi-niche** strategy, which is the *opposite* of USWM's two-segment commitment. Worth studying as a counterexample for what NOT to do positioning-wise.

## How big a threat

**Low.** Reach 2 ($513M AUM solid), Segment overlap 1 (no NJ/NY presence, no overlap with USWM's two segments), Momentum 2 (six niche surface), Capital backing 1 (founder-led) → **6 → low**.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Multi-niche is the opposite strategy.** USWM has committed to two segments deliberately; Wiser claims six. Both can work, but the *commitment trade-off* matters: USWM gets crisper messaging and deeper specialization within fewer cohorts; Wiser gets broader-funnel addressable market at the cost of less segment-specific depth. Don't drift toward Wiser's pattern.
2. **"Single Again" is an unexpectedly powerful niche.** Divorce-recovery is a real life-event-driven segment most firms don't surface. USWM doesn't need to add this segment — but the pattern (segment-by-life-event, not just demographic) is worth noting as a future option.

The narrowest single takeaway: **niche commitment beats niche menu.** Wiser proves a multi-niche menu can scale to $500M; USWM's two-segment commitment is structurally more defensible at launch stage.
