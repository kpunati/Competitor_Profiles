# Truepoint Wealth Counsel — Summary

> Cincinnati OH fee-only fiduciary RIA, founded **1990** — **$6.39B AUM, 103 employees, 37 advisors, 0 DRPs.** Hero is the **catalog's most explicit anti-wirehouse rhetoric**: contrast table of *"People / Compassion / Your Trust / Return on Life"* **vs.** *"Products / Commissions / Our Bottom Line / Return on Investment."* Tagline: *"This is Life and Wealth, Well Managed."* / *"Helping clients achieve financial peace of mind since 1990."*

## The headline

Truepoint's homepage is **the catalog's strongest example of explicit anti-wirehouse positioning** — and one of the cleanest brand-voice differentiators in this entire 18-firm batch. The 4-vs-4 contrast (People / Compassion / Your Trust / Return on Life vs. Products / Commissions / Our Bottom Line / Return on Investment) is exactly the framing USWM should aspire to: not just "we're fee-only" but a *side-by-side comparison* that names what fee-only is rejecting.

The phrase "Return on Life" (vs. "Return on Investment") is the keeper — it reframes the entire wealth-management value proposition. Most firms compete on investment performance; Truepoint reframes the metric.

Threat to USWM: **low** — different region, HNW/UHNW. Strategic value: **highest copywriting-pattern value in this batch**. The anti-wirehouse contrast structure is directly borrowable for USWM, especially given Kean's wirehouse departure history.

## What this means for our entry

1. **The 4-vs-4 contrast is the most borrowable pattern in this batch.** USWM should consider an explicit side-by-side somewhere on the site:
   - **The big-firm path**: Products · Commissions · Your account is a number · Returns measured in basis points
   - **The USWM path**: Active management · Fee-only · You talk to the people who manage your money · Returns measured against your goals

   Or whatever sharper version of that. The structural pattern (us-vs-them contrast at the hero) is high-impact.

2. **"Return on Life" as a measurement-reframe.** Truepoint subordinates investment returns to life outcomes. USWM's voice ("Built around your life") is the same direction; Truepoint's phrasing is sharper.

3. **Long-history framing.** 1990 founding becomes "since 1990" / "35 years" — a credibility shortcut. USWM's equivalent for now: "Kean — 20+ years at Merrill, JPMorgan, Citi, UBS, AIG." Time-in-industry, not time-in-this-firm.

The narrowest single takeaway: **steal the 4-vs-4 contrast structure for USWM's About or homepage.** It's the cleanest anti-wirehouse rhetorical move in the catalog.
