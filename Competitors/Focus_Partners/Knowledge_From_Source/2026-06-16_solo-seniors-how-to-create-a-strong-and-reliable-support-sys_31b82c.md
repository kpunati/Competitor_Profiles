# Solo Seniors: How to Create a Strong and Reliable Support System

April 30, 2026

![Senior man in wheelchair, with adult son](https://focus-financial.transforms.svdcdn.com/production/images/people/FSC_ElderlyParents_Thumbnail.png?w=1600&h=900&auto=compress%2Cformat&fit=crop&dm=1712079022&s=d67a3b625ad22d3e93aa2dfead06c505)

In this session, Joan Garbow, Aging Life Care Director, explores how individuals aging without a built-in support system can remain independent, protected, and in control through proactive planning. The presentation reframes aging support as something that can be intentionally designed, rather than assumed, long before a crisis occurs.

* * *

Attendees learn why solo aging requires unique considerations across health, finances, and decision-making. We also discuss how small, early steps can significantly reduce risk over time. With real-world insights and practical guidance, this session empowers individuals to build reliable support systems that evolve as their needs change.

**In this session, you gain insights into:**  

-   How aging without a default advocate increases risk, but planning can preserve independence and choice.
-   Why support works best when it’s built around roles (medical, financial, emotional, crisis), not reliance on one person.
-   Why community connections and professional relationships are critical pillars of a strong, resilient aging plan.
