## A Belief in Markets

The market is an effective information processing machine. Each day, the world equity markets process billions of dollars in trades between buyers and sellers, and the real-time information they bring helps set prices. Our portfolios use this information to target stocks and bonds with higher expected returns.

* * *

## Broad Diversification

Capturing the expected returns that the market has to offer requires broad diversification across companies, sectors, and regions. It is not possible to predict which subset of securities will perform well over any period. Our portfolios contain thousands of securities across 48 countries.

* * *

## Tax Efficiency

Traditional portfolio management techniques that aim to beat the market are often hampered by tax inefficiency due to high portfolio turnover. We take a systematic approach that improves tax efficiency by limiting the need for frequent trading.

* * *

## Low Costs

Fees and other costs are some of the best predictors of future investment performance. When selecting financial products for portfolio construction, we aim to keep costs as low as possible so that you keep more of your investment returns.

What are “Investable Assets”?

The term ‘Investable Assets’ includes all liquid and near-liquid assets, including brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, RRSPs, TFSAs, trusts, etc.

It does not include the value of your home, properties, equity in a business, etc. They represent assets we can invest in on your behalf.

We assist clients in finding and funding a good life. Our portfolio management philosophy relies on academic research that demonstrates that markets are efficient and that prices reflect all available information.

The best way to capture what the markets offer is to have a low-cost, broadly diversified portfolio that matches your risk tolerance.
