# Cordant Wealth Partners — Summary

> **Portland OR fee-only fiduciary RIA** specializing in technology employees at major Pacific Northwest + national tech employers — **Intel, Microsoft, Nike, Amazon, Apple** named explicitly on the services page. **$370.5M AUM, 5 advisors, 8 employees, 0 DRPs.** Three stated principles: **fiduciary, fee-only, independent** — with an explicit rejection of the Wall Street bank product shelf. Deep expertise in RSUs, stock options, deferred comp, employer benefits. Tagline: "Discover how we can help lower your taxes + make work optional."

## The headline

Cordant is the West Coast counterpart to Brooklyn FI — same fee-only fiduciary structure, same tech-employee niche, but **regional (Portland) instead of national-virtual**, and **without published tier pricing**. Their positioning is anti-wirehouse: the About page articulates independence as freedom from "the whims of a Wall Street bank" — direct rhetoric, not polite. They name the actual companies their clients work at (Intel, Microsoft, Nike, Amazon, Apple), which is unusually concrete. The "work optional" framing — same phrase Brindle & Bay uses for one of its three personas — appears as a closing CTA on the homepage.

Threat to USWM is **low** — Cordant targets a different region (Pacific Northwest tech) and a different segment (tech employees with equity comp), not USWM's NJ/NY early-adults or 40-50 401(k) holders. Strategic value is the same shape as Brooklyn FI: a teachable model for niche commitment, especially the anti-wirehouse rhetoric, which Kean (with his wirehouse-to-independent arc) could lean into more explicitly.

## What they're doing well (worth studying)

- **Named major employers as targeting language.** "Tech employees at Intel, Microsoft, Nike, Amazon, Apple" is dramatically more specific than "tech professionals." The prospect immediately self-identifies or self-excludes. Borrowable: USWM could name specific employers in NJ/NY whose 401(k) plans we know well (e.g. Verizon, Prudential, Merck — the major NJ employer 401(k) plans) to give the 40-50 segment a similar self-identification cue.
- **Anti-wirehouse rhetoric on the About page.** "Not constrained by the whims of a Wall Street bank" is direct, not polite. Most fee-only RIAs in the catalog soften this language. Cordant doesn't.
- **Three-principle structure.** Fiduciary / Fee-Only / Independent — same triad most fee-only firms claim, but Cordant attaches a one-paragraph explanation to each. Most peers list the labels without elaboration.
- **"Work optional" framing as the outcome promise.** Same phrase Brindle & Bay uses ("Amy & Dave — Work Optional"). For tech employees with significant equity comp, "make work optional" is the actual financial outcome — more concrete than generic "retirement" framing.
- **Ebook as the front-funnel.** Lead magnet without a hard-pitch CTA — soft entry point that builds the email list.

## Where they're vulnerable

- **No published pricing.** Even AUM-based firms in the catalog typically publish a tier table or a "we charge X% on the first $Y" disclosure. Cordant doesn't, on the captured pages.
- **Single-office regional concentration.** NW Portland is the only stated location. "Tech employees at Intel/Microsoft/Nike/Amazon/Apple" implies multi-state delivery (Apple = California, Microsoft = Washington, Amazon = Washington primary + many), but the firm's brand is anchored in Portland.
- **No 401(k) optimization surface, no early-adult surface.** Same gaps as most niche-tech firms in the catalog.
- **Site IA not well-captured.** Extract.ts got only the homepage; /services worked but /about and /team returned 404 to our URL guesses. This is a Cordant-side issue (their URL pattern is non-obvious) that limits public-facing discoverability for non-prospects.
- **Narrow-niche dependency.** Same as Brooklyn FI — tech equity comp pipeline is cyclical.

## What this means for our entry

1. **Name specific employers in USWM's segment messaging.** Cordant names Intel/Microsoft/Nike/Amazon/Apple; USWM could name Verizon / Prudential / Merck / J&J / RWJBarnabas — the major NJ employer 401(k)s. Immediate self-identification for the 40-50 segment. This is the cheapest specificity move available.
2. **Lean into Kean's wirehouse-to-independent arc as anti-wirehouse credibility.** Cordant says "not constrained by Wall Street." Kean *was* at Merrill Lynch, JPMorgan Chase, Citi, UBS, and AIG. He's not abstractly anti-wirehouse — he left them. That's a stronger credibility story than Cordant's institutional framing. USWM should put that explicitly on the About page: *"Kean spent 20+ years at Merrill, JPMorgan, Citi, UBS, and AIG. He left to build USWM the way the big firms wouldn't let him."*
3. **"Work optional" is a usable outcome frame.** For USWM's 40-50 mid-career 401(k) segment, "make work optional" is more concrete than "retire confidently." Worth A/B testing in headline copy on the 40-50 landing page.
4. **Publish at least one number.** Cordant's missing pricing is a vulnerability. USWM should beat this by publishing — even a single sentence ("We charge 1% AUM with no minimum; planning-only engagements start at $X") beats the silent gate.

The narrowest single takeaway: **name the employers your 40-50 segment works at.** Cordant proves how much specificity that single move buys.
